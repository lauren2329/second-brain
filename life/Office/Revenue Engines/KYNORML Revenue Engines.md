# KYNORML Consumer Value & Revenue Engines

## KYNORML Core Value Proposition

KYNORML is a member-driven 501(c)(4) organization advancing policy, community, and economic opportunity around wellness and cannabis. Members gain:
- Political influence and advocacy power
- Community belonging and networking
- Credibility and positioning
- Economic opportunity (business partnerships, sponsorships)
- Exclusive member benefits

---

## Revenue Engines

### 1. Memberships
**Type:** Recurring subscription  
**Model:** Individual and organizational membership tiers  
**Consumer Value:** Voting rights, legislative influence, community access, exclusive benefits  
**Revenue Structure:**
- Individual: $50-250/year (basic, sustaining, patron)
- Organization/Business: $500-5000/year
- Revenue: Direct membership dues

### 2. Directory
**Type:** Member directory & business listing  
**Model:** Membership includes listing; premium placements/advertising available  
**Consumer Value:** 
- For members: Credibility and visibility through KYNORML endorsement
- For business members: Direct customer/partner discovery
**Revenue Structure:**
- Premium listing/sponsorship: $100-500/month
- Featured placement: $500-2000/month
- Revenue: Directory sponsorship and advertising

### 3. Merchandise
**Type:** Branded products  
**Model:** KYNORML-branded items (apparel, accessories, goods)  
**Consumer Value:** Community identity, statement of values, support for organization  
**Revenue Structure:**
- Member discounts on bulk orders
- Retail markup on sales
- Bulk sales to corporate sponsors
- Revenue: Product markup and licensing

### 4. Conference
**Type:** Annual membership conference/summit  
**Model:** Multi-day event with keynotes, workshops, networking, policy briefings  
**Consumer Value:**
- Policy education and legislative intelligence
- Network expansion
- Professional development
- Community gathering
**Revenue Structure:**
- Early bird conference registration: $100-200
- Standard registration: $150-300
- Group/member discounts available
- Sponsor booths and packages: $2k-10k
- Revenue: Ticket sales + sponsor fees

### 5. Email
**Type:** Email list monetization  
**Model:** Sponsored email campaigns, newsletter advertising, member announcements  
**Consumer Value:**
- For members: Direct legislative updates and opportunities
- For sponsors: Targeted audience access
**Revenue Structure:**
- Newsletter sponsorships: $500-2000/month
- Sponsored campaigns: $1k-5k per campaign
- Member email list access for approved partners
- Revenue: Email sponsorships and advertiser fees

### 6. Sponsorship (Organization Level)
**Type:** Corporate/organizational partnership sponsorships  
**Model:** Organizations sponsor KYNORML as a whole (annual commitment)  
**Consumer Value:**
- For sponsors: Brand association, member access, policy influence
- For members: Sponsored programs, resources
**Revenue Structure:**
- Sponsorship tiers: $5k-50k+/year
- Benefits: Logo placement, speaking opportunities, member benefits, advisory role
- Multi-year commitments (2-3 year discounts)
- Revenue: Annual sponsorship fees

### 7. Social Media
**Type:** Content and community monetization  
**Model:** 
- Sponsored content posts
- Affiliate revenue from recommendations
- Monetized videos/long-form content
**Consumer Value:**
- Free access to legislative news, community stories, wellness info
- Engagement and visibility for members
**Revenue Structure:**
- Sponsored posts: $500-2000/post
- Affiliate commissions: 5-20% on partner products/services
- Creator fund revenue (YouTube, TikTok): CPM-based
- Revenue: Sponsorships, affiliate commissions, platform revenue

### 8. Sponsors (Activity-Level)
**Type:** Event and program-specific sponsors  
**Model:** Sponsors fund specific KYNORML activities, workshops, or campaigns  
**Consumer Value:**
- For sponsors: Targeted member engagement, named association with cause
- For members: Sponsored programs and events
**Revenue Structure:**
- Activity sponsorship: $1k-10k per event/program
- Named sponsorships (e.g., "Presented by [Company]")
- Exclusive sponsor benefits (booth, speaking slot, attendee data)
- Revenue: Activity sponsorship fees

### 9. Legislative Session Engagement
**Type:** Policy/advocacy services during legislative sessions  
**Model:**
- Legislative tracking and briefing services
- Advocacy campaign coordination
- Member mobilization (calls, lobbying visits, testimony)
- Policy education workshops
**Consumer Value:**
- Real-time legislative intelligence
- Opportunity to influence policy
- Training on advocacy and civic engagement
- Coordinated member power
**Revenue Structure:**
- Premium tier membership add-on: $20-50/month during session
- Corporate advocate access: $500-2000/month
- Paid advocacy campaign support: $5k-50k/campaign
- Workshop fees: $50-200/person
- Revenue: Premium membership tiers, corporate advocacy packages, training fees

### 10. Activity Sponsors
**Type:** Discrete activity and program sponsorship (overlaps with #8 but more granular)  
**Model:** Fund specific workshops, webinars, panels, community gatherings  
**Consumer Value:**
- For sponsors: Direct engagement with interested audience
- For members: Free or low-cost programs and learning
**Revenue Structure:**
- Workshop sponsorship: $500-3000
- Panel/speaker event: $1k-5k
- Community gathering (gaming, social): $500-2000
- Exclusive sponsor benefits (branding, data, access)
- Revenue: Activity sponsorship and naming rights

## Consumer Value Mapping

### For Individual Members
- **Political Power:** Collective advocacy force (legislative session, activity sponsorship)
- **Community:** Belonging, networking, events (conference, social media, gatherings)
- **Benefit Capture:** Access to sponsors' offers, exclusive member directory, merchandise discounts
- **Economic Opportunity:** Business directory exposure, sponsorship opportunities

### For Business/Organizational Members
- **Customer Access:** Member directory, email list, social media reach
- **Brand Association:** Policy alignment, cause marketing
- **Policy Influence:** Sponsorship benefits, advisory roles
- **Networking:** Conference and event access

### For Corporate Sponsors
- **Audience Access:** Targeted engagement with KYNORML members
- **Brand Positioning:** Association with cause, policy agenda
- **Data & Insights:** Member engagement, feedback, market intelligence
- **Influence:** Advisory roles, policy development participation

## Implementation Priority

### Phase 1 (Immediate: 0-3 months)
1. **Memberships** - Core foundation
2. **Directory** - Leverage existing members
3. **Activity Sponsors** - Low friction, existing activities
4. **Email** - Quick monetization of existing list

### Phase 2 (Near-term: 3-6 months)
1. **Sponsorships** - Build on Phase 1 relationships
2. **Merchandise** - Test member demand
3. **Social Media** - Grow audience + monetize

### Phase 3 (Growth: 6-12 months)
1. **Conference** - Annual flagship event
2. **Legislative Session** - Premium services during policy windows
3. **Partnerships** - Deepen sponsor relationships

## Notes

- **Core Revenue Drivers:** Memberships, Sponsorship, Conference, Legislative Services
- **Support Revenue:** Email, Social Media, Merchandise, Directory
- **Growth Strategy:** Lock in core membership base → leverage for sponsor access → grow event/content revenue
- **Member Lifecycle:** Individual → Sustaining Member → Business Member → Sponsor
