# KYNORML: 5 Revenue Programs

## What KYNORML Does

Educates consumers, advocates for patients, connects businesses with consumers, and convenes Kentucky's cannabis community.

Everything you charge for funds one of those four activities.

---

## The 5 Revenue Programs

### 1. MEMBERSHIPS (Individual)
**Supporter** $50/year  
**Advocate** $100/year  
**Champion** $250/year

**Purpose:** Fund advocacy and education

**Deliverables:**
- Legislative monitoring and alerts
- Consumer advocacy and engagement
- Monthly newsletter + action alerts
- Annual conference access
- Community

---

### 2. BUSINESS MEMBERSHIPS
**Business Member** $500/year  
**Supporting Business Member** $1,000/year  
**Partner Business Member** $2,500/year

**Purpose:** Connect businesses with consumers

**What Businesses Pay For:**
- Consumer visibility
- Referrals to qualified customers
- Credibility through KY NORML association
- Promotion and community engagement
- Conference access

**KY NORML Deliverables:**
- Directory listing
- Business promotion (newsletter, social, email, referrals)
- Conference recognition
- Spotlight campaigns and features

---

### 3. SPONSORSHIPS
**Community Sponsor** $500/year  
**Bronze Sponsor** $1,000/year  
**Silver Sponsor** $2,500/year  
**Gold Sponsor** $5,000/year  
**Platinum Sponsor** $10,000/year

**Purpose:** Underwrite KY NORML programs

**What Sponsors Pay For:**
- Direct program funding
- Brand visibility and association
- Conference recognition
- Year-round visibility

**KY NORML Deliverables:**
- Website/newsletter recognition
- Conference recognition (tier-based)
- Featured placement (tier-based)
- Strategic partnership designation (Platinum)

---

### 4. CONFERENCE
**Attendee Ticket** $99-$199  
**Sponsor** $1,000-$10,000+

**Purpose:** Educate, network, and convene community

**Attendee Deliverables:**
- Educational sessions
- Policy updates
- Networking opportunities

**Sponsor Deliverables:**
- Booth/visibility at event
- Speaking opportunity
- Member engagement
- Sponsor recognition

---

### 5. DONATIONS
**One-Time or Monthly** Any amount

**Purpose:** Fund programs that don't generate revenue

**Programs Funded:**
- Consumer education (public resources, guides)
- Consumer advocacy (legislative engagement, monitoring)
- Community access (scholarships, outreach, public participation)

---

## Channels vs. Revenue Engines

**Revenue Engines** (what you charge for):
1. Memberships
2. Business Memberships
3. Sponsorships
4. Conference
5. Donations

**Delivery Channels** (how you fulfill what you charge for):
- Email (newsletter, alerts, member updates)
- Social media (promotion, visibility, community)
- Blog/content (education, consumer guides)
- Directory (business listing, referrals)
- Website (member portal, resources)
- Events/workshops (community, education)

---

## What Consistency Means

You only need to be consistent with what you're **charging for**.

**High Priority (Revenue Critical):**
- **Email:** Monthly newsletter + action alerts (members paying for this)
- **Directory maintenance:** Business members paying for visibility
- **Business promotion:** Newsletter features, social posts, referrals (business members paying for this)
- **Conference planning:** Annual event (revenue + major member benefit)

**Support Priority (Fulfillment):**
- Social media (promotes email, drives directory referrals)
- Blog/content (supports member education and consumer advocacy)
- Website (hosts directory, member resources)

---

## Operational Focus (2 People)

**Core Operations:**
1. **Email workflow** — Monthly newsletter template + action alerts (automate sending, batch write)
2. **Business member promotion** — Monthly business spotlight, quarterly features, ongoing referrals (maintain spreadsheet, batch promotion)
3. **Conference planning** — Annual event logistics (quarterly planning touchpoints)
4. **Member communication** — Tier-based benefits delivery (track what's owed, automate where possible)

**Supporting Operations:**
1. Social media — Promotes the above (automate scheduling)
2. Website/directory — Self-service business updates (minimize maintenance)
3. Community events — Ad-hoc based on capacity
