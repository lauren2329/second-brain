# KYNORML Consumer Value → Revenue Engines

## Consumer Value Thesis

KYNORML provides accessible wellness education on physical and mental health, with cannabis positioned as a legitimate health topic. Consumers seek:
- Credible health information (without judgment)
- Community and connection around wellness
- Access to vetted practitioners and products
- Events and learning experiences
- Practical tools and resources

---

## Active Revenue Engines

### 1. Portal to Wellness
- **Type:** Affiliate Directory
- **Model:** Commission on referred sales → Wonderland Wellness World
- **Consumer Value:** Curated wellness products and services
- **URL:** portaltowellness.org

### 2. Gaming Lounge
- **Type:** Charitable Gaming
- **Model:** Revenue share on gaming activities
- **Consumer Value:** Community gathering space + entertainment
- **Entity:** Common Good Network (c3)

---

## Pipeline Revenue Engines

### 3. Wellness Directory
- **Type:** Premium Directory Listings
- **Model:** Tiered directory of vetted practitioners (therapists, wellness coaches, MDs, etc.) + featured listings
- **Consumer Value:** Trusted practitioner discovery + quality vetting
- **Revenue:** Provider listing fees ($50-500/month), featured placement, certification badges
- **Owner:** CGN (educational, non-clinical positioning)

### 4. Annual Wellness Conference
- **Type:** Conference/Summit
- **Model:** Ticket sales, vendor booths, sponsorships, educational tracks
- **Consumer Value:** Multi-day learning event + community + networking
- **Revenue:** $50-150 tickets, vendor booth fees ($2k-10k), corporate sponsorships
- **Owner:** CGN or KYNORML (educational positioning)

### 5. Certification Program
- **Type:** Professional Training & Certification
- **Model:** Train wellness educators/coaches in KYNORML curriculum (cannabis + holistic wellness)
- **Consumer Value:** Credentialed expertise + career pathway
- **Revenue:** Course tuition ($500-2k per course), certification maintenance fees
- **Owner:** KYNORML or CGN

### 6. Membership Community
- **Type:** Subscription Community
- **Model:** Monthly/annual membership for access to educational content, events, community forums
- **Consumer Value:** Ongoing learning + belonging + exclusive content
- **Revenue:** $15-50/month tiers (basic, premium, professional)
- **Owner:** CGN

### 7. Digital Courses & Content
- **Type:** Online Education
- **Model:** Self-paced courses on wellness topics (mental health, chronic illness, cannabis education, etc.)
- **Consumer Value:** On-demand credible learning
- **Revenue:** $29-199 per course, bulk/institutional licensing
- **Owner:** CGN

### 8. Publishing
- **Type:** Books/Guides/Workbooks
- **Model:** Publish wellness education guides, workbooks, resource manuals
- **Consumer Value:** Tangible reference materials
- **Revenue:** Print sales, eBook sales, wholesale to practitioners
- **Owner:** CGN

### 9. Corporate Wellness Consulting
- **Type:** B2B Services
- **Model:** Consult with employers on wellness programming, cannabis policies, employee education
- **Consumer Value:** For employers: compliant wellness programs; for employees: trustworthy education
- **Revenue:** Consulting fees ($5k-50k projects), licensing programs
- **Owner:** KYNORML

### 10. Practitioner Tools/SaaS
- **Type:** Software Platform
- **Model:** Software for wellness practitioners to manage clients, schedule, reference materials
- **Consumer Value:** For practitioners: operational tool; for consumers: better coordinated care
- **Revenue:** Subscription SaaS ($50-500/month per provider)
- **Owner:** Verdant Ventures LLC

### 11. Merchandise
- **Type:** Branded Products
- **Model:** Wellness-themed merchandise (journals, water bottles, apparel, etc.)
- **Consumer Value:** Community identity + functional wellness tools
- **Revenue:** Retail markup on branded goods
- **Owner:** Verdant Ventures LLC

### 12. Advocacy & Policy Consulting
- **Type:** Government/Policy Services
- **Model:** Advise states/municipalities on cannabis policy, wellness curriculum standards
- **Consumer Value:** Better policy = more accessible education/services
- **Revenue:** Government contracts, foundation grants
- **Owner:** Kentucky NORML (c4)

### 13. Research & Data
- **Type:** Insights/Benchmarking
- **Model:** Publish research on wellness trends, cannabis attitudes, practitioner outcomes
- **Consumer Value:** Evidence-based insights
- **Revenue:** Report sales, institutional subscriptions, foundation grants
- **Owner:** CGN or KYNORML

### 14. Events & Workshops
- **Type:** Local Workshops/Webinars
- **Model:** Recurring local classes, webinars, community events (lower-cost than conference)
- **Consumer Value:** Regular learning opportunities
- **Revenue:** $10-30 per ticket or free (donation-supported)
- **Owner:** CGN

### 15. Partnerships & Sponsorships
- **Type:** Corporate Partnerships
- **Model:** Sponsorship deals with aligned brands, corporate education partnerships
- **Consumer Value:** Better integrated resources
- **Revenue:** Sponsorship fees, revenue share
- **Owner:** All entities

## Quick Wins (Low Effort, High Value)

1. **Wellness Directory** — List existing practitioners already in your network, charge for featured placement
2. **Local Workshops** — Free/donation model workshops in partnership with Gaming Lounge
3. **Membership Launch** — Wrap existing content + community access into tier (basic free, premium $20/mo)
4. **Corporate Partnerships** — Approach local employers about sponsoring workshops
5. **Merchandise** — Start with one item (journal, sticker pack) testing brand attachment

---

## Notes

- **Positioning:** All engines frame consumer value first (health, learning, community) before monetization
- **Tax Treatment:** Ensure c3/c4/LLC alignment per each engine's legal structure
- **Compliance:** Education-first messaging maintains nonprofit credibility
- **Cross-Promotion:** Each engine feeds others (conference → membership, directory → partnerships, etc.)
