# Sales Playbook: Dispensary Partnerships

## Sales Person's Job

Close dispensary partnership deals (any tier: directory → annual partnership). Track pipeline. Nurture relationships. Hit monthly revenue target.

---

## Revenue Target

**Monthly target per sales person:** $3,000-5,000 in new partnership revenue

**Examples to hit target:**
- 5 directory listings @ $150/mo = $750/mo
- 2 monthly sponsors @ $500/mo = $1,000/mo
- 1 event sponsor @ $3,000 = $3,000/mo
- OR: 1 annual partnership @ $5,000 = $5,000/mo

---

## Weekly Workflow

### Monday (Prospecting - 2-3 hours)
**Task:** Identify + reach out to 5-10 dispensaries

**Process:**
1. List all dispensaries in target area (Google Maps, Weedmaps, local directories)
2. Find owner/manager email/phone (LinkedIn, website, call)
3. Send personalized email pitch:
   - Subject: "[Dispensary Name] + KYNORML Partnership"
   - 3-4 sentences
   - Link to dispensary partnership pitch doc
   - CTA: "15-min call this week?"
4. Log in spreadsheet: Name, Contact, Date Contacted, Status

**Example email:**
> Hi [Owner/Manager],
> 
> We help dispensaries build trust and reach engaged customers through KYNORML. Our 2,000+ members actively seek out recommended businesses. We have partnership options from $100-5,000/month.
> 
> Would you be open to a quick 15-min call to see if there's a fit?
> 
> [Link to pitch doc]

---

### Tuesday (Follow-Up Calls - 2-3 hours)
**Task:** Call warm leads from previous week + Monday responses

**Process:**
1. Check email for responses from Monday outreach
2. Call anyone who responded (or didn't respond but opened email)
3. 15-min call script:

   **Opening:**
   "Thanks for your time. Quick question: Are you looking to reach more engaged wellness customers right now?"
   
   **Listen. If yes:**
   "We have five partnership options. Which sounds most relevant: a simple directory listing, ongoing social visibility, or something more hands-on?"
   
   **If interested:**
   "Let me send over a few case studies and member demographics. When can we jump on a call to discuss?"
   
   **If not interested:**
   "No problem. Can I check back in 6 months?"

4. Log outcome: Interested / Not interested / Follow up later

---

### Wednesday (Demos/Consultations - 2-3 hours)
**Task:** Run calls with interested prospects

**Process:**
1. Screen share the pitch doc + case studies
2. Ask: "What's your biggest customer acquisition challenge right now?"
3. Map their challenge to tier:
   - Small budget + want discovery → Directory listing
   - Want consistent visibility → Monthly sponsor
   - Have a launch/promo → Event sponsor
   - Deep integration → Workshop or annual
4. Send agreement + next steps

---

### Thursday (Closed Deals - 1 hour)
**Task:** Process paperwork, onboard new partners

**Process:**
1. Get signed agreement
2. Process payment (invoice or card)
3. Send welcome email with what happens next
4. Log in tracking spreadsheet

---

### Friday (Pipeline Review - 1 hour)
**Task:** Review week, update forecast

**Process:**
1. Check spreadsheet: How many contacted? Interested? Closed?
2. Calculate: On track for $3-5k/month?
3. If behind: Plan extra prospecting next week

## Sales Targets

**Per Month:**
- Contacts made: 40-50 dispensaries
- Interested: 10-15 (25% conversion)
- Closed: 3-5 deals
- Revenue: $3,000-5,000

**Per Year:**
- 36-60 closed deals
- $36,000-60,000 revenue

---

## What Closes Deals

1. **Social proof** — "Here's what [Other Dispensary] gets from this"
2. **Clarity** — No confusion about what they're paying for
3. **Speed** — Close in 1-2 weeks, not months
4. **Relationship** — You remember their name, check in

---

## What Kills Deals

1. **Unclear value** — They don't know what they're paying for
2. **Slow response** — They ask a question, you reply in 5 days
3. **Pushy** — Feeling like they're being sold to, not consulted
4. **No follow-up** — You pitch once, never call back

---

## Messaging Consistency

**Use this language every time:**
- "Engaged members" (not "users" or "customers")
- "Vetted partners" (why members trust you)
- "Member referrals" (outcome, not features)
- "Partnership tiers" (not "packages" or "plans")

---

## Red Flags (Don't Waste Time)

- They only care about "direct sales" (KYNORML isn't that)
- They want to negotiate price below tier (stick to tiers)
- They ghost you for 2 weeks after saying "interested" (move on)
- They want custom features KYNORML can't deliver

---

## Success Looks Like

- Hitting $3-5k revenue target monthly
- 3-5 closed deals per month
- Pipeline always has 10-15 warm leads
- Repeat partnerships (annual tiers converting to renewals)
- Referrals from existing partners (best lead source)
