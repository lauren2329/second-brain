# KYNORML Operations: Focus on Revenue Programs

## Principle

Only automate and systematize what you're charging for. Everything else is secondary.

---

## The 4 Things That Must Be Consistent

### 1. MEMBERSHIPS (Monthly Newsletter + Action Alerts)
**Why consistent matters:** Members pay to stay informed. Sporadic email = churn.

**What to deliver:**
- Monthly newsletter (legislative updates, member news, community highlights)
- Action alerts (legislative opportunities, calls to action) - frequency varies
- Member-only content (exclusive benefits, early event access)

**Automation:**
- Email platform: Mailchimp or ConvertKit (automate sends, templates)
- Calendar: 1st of month = newsletter goes out (automated)
- Alert system: Setup forwarding/automation for legislative alerts

**VMP owns:**
- Write newsletter (2-3 hours, once/month)
- Decide when alerts go out (as news breaks)
- Member communication (urgent issues)

**Frequency:** 1 email/month guaranteed + alerts as needed

---

### 2. BUSINESS MEMBER PROMOTION (Directory + Visibility)
**Why consistent matters:** Businesses pay for visibility and referrals. Inconsistent promotion = cancelled memberships.

**What to deliver:**
- Business directory (searchable, up-to-date)
- Monthly newsletter mention (rotating featured business)
- Quarterly spotlight campaign (email + social)
- Social media posts (business mentions, referrals)
- Member referrals (recommend to consumers)

**Automation:**
- Directory platform: Self-service portal (Airtable + Webflow or similar)
- Social scheduling: Buffer or Meta Business Suite
- Newsletter template: Built-in business feature

**VMP owns:**
- Maintain directory (handle listing updates, vetting)
- Write monthly spotlight (1 business feature)
- Respond to referral requests
- Create quarterly social campaigns (batch posts)
- Track and deliver promised promotions

**Frequency:** 1 monthly newsletter feature + quarterly spotlight + ongoing referrals + 4x/month social mentions (minimum)

---

### 3. CONFERENCE (Annual Event)
**Why consistent matters:** Major revenue generator + member benefit. Success requires planning.

**What to deliver:**
- Educational programming (speakers, sessions)
- Networking opportunities
- Sponsor recognition
- Member access/discount

**Automation:**
- Registration platform: Eventbrite
- Sponsor comms: Email templates
- Schedule: Auto-published

**VMP owns:**
- Speaker recruitment (3-6 months before)
- Sponsor prospecting (3-6 months before)
- Venue/logistics coordination
- Day-of event management
- Post-event follow-up

**Timeline:**
- 6 months before: Start speaker/sponsor recruiting
- 3 months before: Finalize program, open registration
- 1 month before: Confirm all details
- Day-of: Run event
- 2 weeks after: Thank yous and follow-up

---

### 4. BUSINESS MEMBER FULFILLMENT (What You Promise)
**Why consistent matters:** You're charging for tangible benefits. Not delivering = refunds + reputation damage.

**Tier-based commitments (track and deliver):**

**Business Member ($500/year):**
- ✓ Directory listing
- ✓ New member announcement (email + social)
- ✓ One annual spotlight
- ✓ One annual newsletter feature
- ✓ Four social media mentions

**Supporting Business Member ($1,000/year):**
- ✓ Everything above, plus
- ✓ Featured directory placement
- ✓ Four newsletter mentions
- ✓ Four quarterly spotlight campaigns
- ✓ Priority referrals

**Partner Business Member ($2,500/year):**
- ✓ Everything above, plus
- ✓ Category sponsorship
- ✓ Monthly visibility campaign (12x/year)
- ✓ Annual partner profile article
- ✓ Premium conference recognition

**Automation:**
- Tracking spreadsheet: Track what's been delivered per member
- Calendar: Auto-reminders when annual benefits reset
- Batch scheduling: Quarterly spotlights, monthly mentions

**VMP owns:**
- Maintaining fulfillment tracker
- Ensuring every promised benefit is delivered
- Following up on missed deliverables

**Frequency:** Track monthly, deliver on schedule

---

## What NOT to Worry About (Yet)

These are secondary:
- Daily social media posts
- Weekly blog posts
- Regular workshops/events
- Community building content
- Legislative session tooling
- Merchandise

These support the core 4, but aren't revenue-critical. Do them when you have capacity.

---

## Weekly Workflow (VMP + Leadership)

### Monday (Business Member Promotion - 1 hour)
- Review spotlight schedule
- Check for referral requests
- Respond to directory update requests

### Tuesday (Newsletter Planning - 1 hour)
- Decide on this month's business feature
- Gather member news/updates
- Start newsletter draft

### Wednesday (Social & Email Batch - 2 hours)
- Batch create quarterly spotlight social posts (if quarter starts)
- Schedule business mentions in Buffer (4 posts for month)
- Finalize and schedule newsletter for Friday send

### Thursday (Member/Business Communication - 1 hour)
- Respond to membership inquiries
- Handle business member questions
- Track fulfillment obligations

### Friday (Send & Review - 1 hour)
- Send newsletter (automated)
- Review week: Did everything get done?
- Track on fulfillment spreadsheet

## Success = Delivery

You're not judged on how "active" you are on social media or how many blog posts you write.

You're judged on:
- **Do members get their monthly newsletter?** (Consistently, reliably)
- **Do business members get their promised visibility?** (On schedule, tracked)
- **Does the conference happen?** (Annually, on time, professional)
- **Do memberships renew?** (High retention = consistent delivery)

That's it. Deliver those 4 things consistently, and the revenue flows.

---

## Anti-Burnout Structure

**Pre-batch everything:**
- Monthly newsletters: Write 3 months at once (6 hours, quarterly task)
- Quarterly spotlights: Batch write 4 spotlights at once (4 hours, quarterly)
- Social posts: Batch schedule monthly mentions (2 hours, monthly)

**Automate sends:**
- Newsletter goes out automatically (first Friday of month)
- Social posts schedule automatically
- Renewal reminders sent automatically

**Result:** Even if VMP is overwhelmed, scheduled content still goes out. The engines keep running.
