## Overview

Every NORML state chapter has access to a full library of national resources — content, graphics, newsletters, action alerts, campaigns, and more — at no cost. This playbook defines how KYNORML uses those resources and how to avoid the most common mistake: letting national NORML's donation links replace KYNORML's own fundraising.

This playbook is also part of the **NORML Chapter Replication Box** — every chapter should implement this system.

## The Donation Distinction — Critical

**The problem:** National NORML's emails, website, and content all contain donation links. When KYNORML reposts or shares national content without addressing this, Kentucky supporters click those links and donate to the national organization — not to KYNORML.

This is not a bad thing for the movement. It is a cash flow problem for KYNORML.

**Why the distinction matters:**
- National NORML is a separate 501(c)(4) organization based in Washington, D.C.
- KYNORML is an independent Kentucky chapter — a separate legal entity with separate finances
- Donations to national NORML do not fund KYNORML operations, events, or advocacy
- National NORML's resources support all 50 states; KYNORML's work is Kentucky-specific

**The explanation to use with supporters:**

> "When you donate to national NORML, you're supporting the national movement — federal lobbying, research, and resources shared across all 50 states. That matters.
>
> When you donate to KYNORML, you're funding Kentucky-specific work — our advocacy at the state legislature, KYCannaCon, local patient resources, and the team on the ground in Kentucky.
>
> Both matter. Both are separate. KYNORML is your Kentucky chapter."

**Apply this explanation in:**
- Any email that shares or references national NORML content
- Social posts that share national NORML links
- The KYNORML website FAQ or About page
- New subscriber welcome email

## National Email Newsletters — How to Use Them

### News of the Week (Thursdays)

National NORML sends a weekly news roundup every Thursday.

**KYNORML use:**
1. Review Thursday's email for stories with Kentucky relevance
2. Identify 1–2 stories to repurpose for the KY audience
3. Add to that week's social batch (Track 2) with a Kentucky lens
4. If a story is significant (KY legislation, major federal move), trigger an email to the 8k list (Track 1 — news reaction campaign) or a PR response (Track 4)

**Always pair repurposed national content with a KYNORML donation CTA or action item.** Never share national content alone without adding a Kentucky layer and a KYNORML link.

### Weekend Weed Read (Saturdays)

National NORML sends a longer newsletter every Saturday.

**KYNORML use:**
1. Review for educational content, cannabis culture, and policy updates
2. Repurpose for social media — especially the educational pillar (Track 2)
3. Useful source material for the monthly education email to the 8k list
4. Do not forward the full national newsletter to your list — curate, add KY context, and always include a KYNORML link

## Action Alerts System

National NORML prepares action alerts when federal legislation moves — urging supporters to contact their representatives.

**What KYNORML does with action alerts:**

1. **Receive the alert** from national NORML
2. **Localize it** — identify the specific Kentucky federal and state legislators relevant to the bill
3. **Send to the 8k list** via Action Network with a Kentucky-specific message:
   > "National NORML is calling on all cannabis supporters to take action on [bill]. For Kentucky, that means contacting [specific senators/representatives]. Here's how."
4. **Post to social** with the action link and KY legislator tags
5. **Track participation** — how many Action Network emails were sent from Kentucky

**Each state chapter should have a standard message for their federal delegation.** Build this once, update as legislators change.

### Kentucky Federal Delegation Template

| Legislator | Office | Contact Method | NORML Position |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Senator 1] | U.S. Senate | [contact link] | [support / oppose / persuadable] |
| [Senator 2] | U.S. Senate | [contact link] | [support / oppose / persuadable] |
| [Representatives] | U.S. House | [contact links] | [support / oppose / persuadable] |

**Update this table every legislative session.**

## Smoke the Vote

National NORML's voter engagement campaign. Runs during election cycles.

**What it is:** A campaign to register cannabis supporters to vote and mobilize them around candidates and ballot measures that affect cannabis policy.

**KYNORML participation:**

1. **Register** KYNORML's participation with national NORML at campaign launch
2. **Promote** Smoke the Vote registration links to the 8k list and social following
3. **Localize** — identify Kentucky-specific races and ballot measures where cannabis is a factor
4. **Report back** — share KYNORML's participation numbers with national NORML

**Timing:** Align with primary and general election cycles. Begin 60 days before voter registration deadlines.

**Integration with email + social:**
- Dedicated Smoke the Vote email to 8k list (Action Network)
- Social posts using national NORML Smoke the Vote graphics
- Add KY voter registration link + KY-specific candidate info

## Press Release Exchange

National NORML and state chapters maintain a mutual press release relationship.

**What KYNORML sends to national:**
- Every KYNORML press release, same day it's published
- KYCannaCon announcements and recaps
- Legislative victories or defeats specific to Kentucky
- Notable patient stories (with permission)
- Event recaps

**How to send:**
- Email to national NORML press contact: **[ADD CONTACT]**
- Subject line format: `[STATE PRESS RELEASE] KYNORML — [Headline]`
- Include: full release, contact info, high-res photo if applicable

**What KYNORML gets in return:**
- National visibility when KY news is notable
- Potential syndication of KY stories on norml.org
- Inclusion in national NORML communications when relevant

**Build and maintain a press release archive** — every KYNORML press release logged by date, topic, and whether it was submitted to national.

## Content Repurposing Rules

These rules apply every time national NORML content is used.

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| Always add a Kentucky lens | National content alone doesn't build KYNORML's local identity |
| Always include a KYNORML CTA | Donation link, event link, or action item — never share national content without one |
| Credit national NORML | "Via @NORML" or "Source: norml.org" — give credit, maintain trust |
| Do not replace your own voice | National content supplements KYNORML content, it doesn't replace it |
| Never forward national donation links without the distinction explanation | Protects KYNORML's fundraising revenue |

## The Chapter Box — Replicable for Other NORML Chapters

This system applies to every NORML state chapter. The national resources are the same for all 50 states — the work is in the localization layer.

**What gets packaged for other chapters:**
1. This playbook (customized to chapter name + state)
2. Donation distinction language template
3. Action alert localization workflow
4. Federal delegation contact tracker template
5. Press release submission process + archive template
6. Smoke the Vote participation checklist
7. Weekly national resource rhythm

**The key insight for other chapters:** National NORML does the heavy lifting on content production. The chapter's job is to localize, add the state lens, protect chapter donations, and participate in national campaigns. The chapter that does this consistently builds authority and donor loyalty faster than one that tries to produce all its own content.

## Chapter Technology Stack

Every NORML chapter should run on the same core tools. This is the standard stack.

### Legislative Tracking

**BillTrack50** (billtrack50.com)
- Tracks cannabis bills at the state and federal level
- Set up alerts for every bill relevant to your state
- Use it to monitor bill status, committee assignments, votes, and amendments
- Action alerts (from national NORML) are triggered by bill movement — BillTrack50 is how you see it coming
- Every chapter director should have this running for their state

### Advocacy + Organizing

**Action Network** (actionnetwork.org)
- Email list management for advocacy sends
- Petition hosting
- Event registration
- Action alert distribution to supporters
- Integrates with legislative campaigns — one-click email to representatives
- Used by KYNORML for the 8k list and all advocacy communications

### Payments + Fundraising

Chapters should choose based on their legal structure and use case:

| Tool | Best For | Fee |
|---|---|---|
| **Zeffy** (zeffy.com) | Nonprofit donations, ticket sales, memberships, merch | 0% platform fee — donors cover Zeffy's costs optionally |
| **ActBlue** (actblue.com) | Political and advocacy fundraising — 501(c)(4)s | 3.95% processing fee |

**Guidance:**
- Use **Zeffy** for conference tickets, memberships, merch, and general donations — 0% fees means more money stays with the chapter
- Use **ActBlue** when the ask is explicitly political — candidate-adjacent campaigns, voter mobilization drives, Smoke the Vote
- Do not use both for the same campaign — pick one per ask to avoid donor confusion

### Tech Stack Summary

| Function | Tool |
|---|---|
| Legislative monitoring | BillTrack50 |
| Advocacy emails + organizing | Action Network |
| Nonprofit payments (donations, tickets, memberships) | Zeffy |
| Political / advocacy fundraising | ActBlue |
| Project and follow-up tracking | Monday.com |
| Social scheduling | Buffer or Meta Business Suite |
| Email newsletter / mass send | Action Network |
| Payment processing (conference vendors / invoices) | QuickBooks or Zeffy |
