## Overview

The problem with social media is daily friction. This system removes it.
One 2-hour batch session every 2 weeks. Content created once, adapted for three platforms, scheduled. Done.
National NORML provides the raw material — you provide the Kentucky lens.

**Platforms: Facebook, Instagram, Threads**
**Scheduling tool: Buffer or Meta Business Suite (both free, both support all three platforms)**

---

## How the Three Platforms Work Together

You are not creating three times the content. You are creating once and adapting the format for each platform's strengths. Most posts start as one idea and become three native versions.

| Platform | What It's For | Format | Posting Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Facebook** | Community hub — events, longer context, shares to groups, the audience that reads | Feed posts with full captions, event listings, photo albums, links | 5–7x/week |
| **Instagram** | Visual storytelling — the audience that scrolls | Feed posts (graphics/photos), Stories (daily, casual), Reels (short video, highest reach) | Feed 4–5x/week, Stories daily |
| **Threads** | Real-time conversation — the audience that reacts | Short text posts, replies, quick takes on news, conversational tone | Daily, can be multiple times/day — lower production lift |

**The practical workflow:** Draft the idea once. Write it long for Facebook. Pull the punchiest line for Instagram caption + pair with a graphic. Cut it to one or two sentences for Threads, written more like a reaction or a thought than an announcement.

**Threads is the lowest lift, highest frequency platform.** It rewards showing up often with short, conversational posts — reactions to news, one-line thoughts, quick polls, replies to others in the cannabis policy conversation. This is where you can post daily without batch-creating graphics.

**Instagram is the highest production platform.** Every feed post needs a visual. Stories can be lower-fidelity (phone photos, behind-the-scenes, text-on-image). Reels get the most reach but take more time — batch 2–3 per two-week cycle, not 14.

**Facebook is the connective tissue.** It's where event details live, where the directory gets shared into community groups, where National NORML content gets reposted with full context, and where press releases and longer announcements get distributed.

## Content Pillars

Every post fits one of five pillars. This prevents blank-page paralysis.

| Pillar | What it is | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Patient Resources** | Directory highlights, how to access KY medical cannabis, patient tips | KYNORML directory, KY cannabis program |
| **Policy + News** | KY legislation updates, national cannabis policy changes | National NORML, KY legislature, news |
| **Education** | Cannabis wellness, science, myths vs. facts | National NORML content (free to use) |
| **Community** | KYCannaCon updates, member spotlights, event recaps, team | Internal |
| **Action** | Donation asks, membership, merch, petition, event tickets | Zeffy links, event registration |

## The Batch Workflow

### When
Every 2 weeks. Block 2.5 hours. Do not create content outside this window — except reactive posts (see below).

### The Session (2.5 hours)

**Hour 1 — Source content (30 min) + Draft core ideas (30 min)**

1. Go to national NORML's social media or website — pull 3–4 recent posts or articles relevant to Kentucky
2. Check KY cannabis news — any bills, program updates, patient stories from the past 2 weeks
3. Check your own calendar — KYCannaCon news, directory highlights, membership pushes, upcoming dates
4. Draft 7 core content ideas using the 2-week pillar rotation below — one idea per content day, NOT one idea per platform

**Hour 1.5 — Adapt each idea for three platforms (45 min)**

For each of the 7 core ideas, write three versions:
- **Facebook version:** Full caption, context, link
- **Instagram version:** Punchy caption (2–4 sentences) + note what graphic/photo pairs with it
- **Threads version:** 1–2 sentences, conversational, reaction-style

**Hour 2 — Format + Schedule (45 min)**

1. Pull or create graphics for Instagram posts (national NORML graphics or Canva — 5–7 visuals total for the 2 weeks)
2. Load Facebook and Instagram posts into scheduling tool with dates/times
3. Load Threads posts into the queue — these can post more frequently, so add 3–5 additional short Threads-only posts (reactions to news, quick thoughts, polls) spaced through the two weeks
4. Done — do not touch social media again except for Stories (see below) and reactive posts

---

## Daily — Outside the Batch (5 minutes)

**Instagram Stories:** Post something once a day. This does not need to be planned in the batch session — it's the casual, behind-the-scenes layer. A photo from the office, a quick thought, a repost of someone tagging KYNORML, a poll. Phone-quality is fine. This is the platform's lowest-lift, highest-connection feature.

**Threads engagement:** Spend 5 minutes replying to relevant conversations in the cannabis policy space — other NORML chapters, advocates, journalists covering the issue. Threads rewards participation, not just posting.

## 2-Week Pillar Rotation (Core Ideas — Adapt Each for All 3 Platforms)

| Day | Pillar | Core Idea |
|---|---|---|
| Mon Week 1 | Patient Resources | Directory highlight — feature one business or resource |
| Wed Week 1 | Policy + News | National NORML policy update with KY context |
| Fri Week 1 | Action | Membership, donation, or directory CTA (match to current revenue push) |
| Sun Week 1 | Education | Cannabis wellness fact or myth-bust |
| Tue Week 2 | Community | KYCannaCon update, member spotlight, or team moment |
| Thu Week 2 | Patient Resources | Directory highlight — different category |
| Sat Week 2 | Policy + News | Week-in-review or KY cannabis news preview |

Seven core ideas. Each becomes a Facebook post, an Instagram post, and a Threads post — three platform-native versions of the same idea. That's 21 scheduled posts from 7 ideas, plus daily Stories and Threads-only reactive content layered in.

## Platform-Specific Post Formats

### Facebook

**Standard post:**
> [1–2 sentence hook — can be longer-form than other platforms]
> [Context, fact, or story — Facebook audiences will read a full paragraph]
> [Direct CTA or link]
> [2–3 hashtags max — Facebook doesn't reward hashtag density like Instagram]

**Where this also goes:** Share into relevant Kentucky and NORML-affiliated Facebook groups (not as a blast — pick 1–2 per post where it's genuinely relevant).

### Instagram

**Feed post caption:**
> [Punchy 1-sentence hook]
> [1–2 sentences of context]
> [CTA — "Link in bio" or direct link if post type allows]
> [Full hashtag set: #KYCannabis #KYNORML #KentuckyNORML #MedicalCannabisKY #CannabisAdvocacy #KentuckyCannabis #NORML #CannabisPatients]

**Stories (daily, casual):** Photo or short video + text overlay. No caption needed. Polls, question stickers, and countdown stickers (for KYCannaCon) drive engagement.

**Reels (2–3 per 2-week cycle):** Short video — repurpose national NORML video content with a KY voiceover or text overlay, or simple talking-to-camera clips on policy updates or event news. This is the highest-reach format; don't skip it even though it's the highest production lift.

### Threads

**Standard post (the bulk of Threads content):**
> [One or two sentences. Conversational. Sounds like a thought, not an announcement.]

Examples of what works on Threads:
> "Kentucky's cannabis bill just cleared committee. Here's what that actually means for patients →"
>
> "Asked 10 people this week if they knew KY has licensed dispensaries now. 6 didn't. We have work to do."
>
> "Hot take: the conversation about cannabis policy in this state is about to get a lot more interesting. More soon."

**Reactive Threads posts:** When cannabis news breaks, Threads is the fastest place to react — faster than drafting a full Facebook post or sourcing an Instagram graphic. Post the reaction on Threads first, then build out the fuller Facebook/Instagram version later if it warrants it.

### Cross-Platform Templates

**Repurposed national NORML content:**
- *Facebook:* "[Tag/credit @NORML] — here's what this means for Kentucky: [1–2 sentences of KY context]. [Link]"
- *Instagram:* Repost the graphic, caption with KY context, credit in caption and tags
- *Threads:* "[One sentence reacting to or amplifying the national point with a KY angle]"

**Directory highlight:**
- *Facebook:* "Spotlight: [Business Name] — [1–2 sentences about what they offer and why they matter to the KY cannabis community]. Find them in the KYNORML directory → [link]"
- *Instagram:* Graphic featuring the business + short caption + "link in bio"
- *Threads:* "Did you know [Business Name] is in our directory? [One sentence on why that matters]"

**Action post (membership, donation, directory, tickets):**
- *Facebook:* Full context on why this ask matters right now + direct link
- *Instagram:* Graphic with the ask + short caption + "link in bio"
- *Threads:* Short, direct: "[The ask in one sentence]. [Link or 'link in bio']"

## Reactive Posts (Outside Batch)

When cannabis news breaks in Kentucky or nationally, post within 24 hours.
See [[Track 4 - PR Response System]] for the full reactive workflow.

For social specifically:
- Draft one post immediately when news breaks
- Use the news as the hook, KYNORML's position as the body
- Link to full PR or KYNORML statement if available
- This is the only time you post outside the batch schedule

## Graphics

**National NORML graphics:** Free to use. Download from national NORML's media resources.
**Canva:** For KY-specific graphics. Keep brand consistent — use KYNORML colors and logo.
**Do not create graphics from scratch** for standard batch posts. Repurpose what exists.

---

## Hashtag Bank

**Facebook:** Use 2–3 max. `#KYNORML #KYCannabis #KentuckyCannabis`
**Instagram:** Use the full set. `#KYNORML #KentuckyNORML #KYCannabis #MedicalCannabisKY #CannabisAdvocacy #KentuckyCannabis #NORML #CannabisPatients`
**Threads:** Hashtags are not part of Threads culture — skip them or use one at most. The platform rewards conversational text, not tags.

**Event-specific (Facebook + Instagram):**
`#KYCannaCon #KentuckyCannabisConference`

---

## Reactive Posts — Where They Go First

When cannabis news breaks in Kentucky or nationally:

1. **Threads first** — fastest format, lowest lift, react within the hour if possible
2. **Facebook second** — fuller context post within 24 hours, links to any KYNORML statement
3. **Instagram if it warrants a graphic** — only for major news that will stay relevant for more than a day

See [[Track 4 - PR Response System]] for the full reactive workflow and how social posts connect to press statements.
