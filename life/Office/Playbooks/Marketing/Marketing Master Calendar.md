## Overview

This is the one view that ties every marketing track together by month: what social content emphasizes, which email campaign goes out, which press moment runs, and which revenue engine gets the push. Everything else (Track 1, Track 2, Track 4, the revenue dashboards) feeds into this calendar — this is where you look first to know what this month's marketing actually is.

**The anchor points of the year:**
- **Kentucky General Assembly session** (January–March/April) — the single biggest driver of relevance, press hooks, and advocacy engagement
- **KYCannaCon** (early March, Year 4) — the signature event; its production cycle dominates September–March
- **Off-season** (April–October) — lighter on press, heavier on recurring revenue engines (membership, directory, donor cultivation)

**How to use this:** At the start of each month, open this file, find the row, and that's your marketing plan for the month — what to post, what to send, what to pitch, what to push. Cross-reference the linked tracks for the actual templates and execution detail.

## The Pattern Underneath the Calendar

**November through March is press-heavy and event-driven.** This is when KYCannaCon production, the legislative session, and the bulk of planned press releases stack on top of each other. Expect higher email frequency, daily Threads activity, and the year's biggest revenue moments (early bird tickets, Presenting Sponsor, event-day sales).

**April through October is recurring-revenue-driven, not press-driven.** Fewer planned press moments — don't force one where there's no real news (a thin release damages credibility more than no release). This window is for building the floor: membership, directory listings, donor cultivation, and chapter outreach (VMP) — engines that compound quietly while the next KYCannaCon and session cycle is being built.

**Reactive content runs year-round, on top of this calendar — not instead of it.** When cannabis news breaks (KY legislation, national NORML statements, dispensary news), the reactive workflow in [[Track 4 - PR Response System]] takes priority for that 24–48 hour window: Threads first, then Facebook, then email alert if it's significant enough. Log it and return to the planned calendar.

## Monthly Checklist — What to Do When You Open This Calendar

1. **Find this month's row.** That's your theme — everything you create this month should reinforce it.
2. **Check the Social Content Emphasis column** — this tells you which of the five content pillars (Patient Resources, Policy + News, Education, Community, Action) to weight heavier in this cycle's batch session. See [[Track 2 - Social Media System]] for the pillar rotation and batch workflow.
3. **Check the Email Send column** — this is the campaign to load into Action Network / Zeffy this month. See [[Track 1 - Email Campaign System]] for the template.
4. **Check the Press Moment column** — if there's a planned release this month, start drafting at least one week ahead using the template in [[Track 4 - PR Response System]]. If the column says "none planned," don't force one.
5. **Check the Revenue Engine Push column** — this is what this month's marketing is actually building toward. Cross-reference [[Revenue Action Dashboard]] and [[Weekly Action Plan]] for the week-by-week execution.
6. **Log everything** — every send, post, release, and revenue result goes in Monday.com per the workflows in each track. The calendar only works if the data feeding back into it is current.
