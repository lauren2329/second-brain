## Overview

Cannabis chapters will face bad news. Legislation fails. Members get arrested. Media misrepresents the organization. The chapters that survive and grow are the ones that respond quickly, calmly, and with a prepared voice — not the ones that go silent or say the wrong thing.

This box ensures every chapter has a response ready before the crisis happens.

---

## Crisis Response Principles

1. **Respond, don't react** — take 2 hours to prepare a response, not 2 minutes to post something you'll regret
2. **One voice** — only the Executive Director or designated spokesperson speaks publicly during a crisis
3. **Facts first** — never speculate. State only what is confirmed.
4. **Acknowledge, don't grovel** — if the chapter made a mistake, acknowledge it plainly and move to what you're doing about it
5. **Protect the mission** — every statement should reinforce that the chapter exists to serve patients and cannabis justice, not to generate drama

## Crisis Scenarios and Responses

---

### Scenario 1: Legislation Fails

**When it happens:** A bill the chapter supported goes down in committee or on the floor.

**What NOT to do:** Post in anger. Call out legislators by name in hostile terms. Go silent and disappear.

**Response steps:**
1. Within 24 hours: Post a statement — calm, factual, forward-looking
2. Email the list — brief update, acknowledge the loss, pivot to next steps
3. Thank supporters publicly for their advocacy

**Statement template:**
> [Bill name] did not pass [chamber] today. We're disappointed — but not deterred.
>
> [X] years of advocacy has taught us that progress on cannabis reform is never a straight line. Kentucky / [state] patients deserve better, and we will continue fighting until they have it.
>
> We thank every patient, advocate, and supporter who contacted their representative. Your voice was heard — and it will be louder next session.
>
> [Chapter Name] will be back at the legislature in [next session year]. The work continues.
>
> — [Executive Director Name], [Chapter Name]

---

### Scenario 2: A Member or Board Member Is Publicly Arrested

**When it happens:** Someone publicly associated with the chapter is arrested — especially on drug-related charges.

**What NOT to do:** Comment on the individual's guilt or innocence. Distance the chapter publicly before you have facts. Defend behavior you don't know the full story on.

**Response steps:**
1. The board convenes within 24 hours — emergency meeting
2. If the individual holds a leadership position: they step down or are suspended from duties pending outcome (this protects the chapter, not a judgment)
3. Prepare a holding statement (see template below)
4. Issue only if media inquires — do not proactively announce

**Holding statement template:**
> [Chapter Name] is aware of the situation involving [first name only or "a chapter member"]. We take matters like this seriously and are reviewing the circumstances.
>
> [Chapter Name]'s mission is advocacy for cannabis patients and policy reform. That mission continues.
>
> We have no further comment at this time.

**If media asks follow-up questions:**
> We respect the legal process and will not comment on an ongoing matter.

---

### Scenario 3: Negative Press Coverage

**When it happens:** A news story misrepresents the chapter, its members, or its position on an issue.

**Response steps:**
1. Read the piece in full before responding to anything
2. Identify specifically what is factually incorrect (not just unfavorable — factually wrong)
3. If factually incorrect: contact the journalist directly with a correction request before going public. Most journalists will correct errors when approached professionally.
4. If the journalist does not correct: issue a public statement of correction
5. If the piece is unfavorable but factually accurate: do not issue a statement — you'll amplify it

**Correction request email:**
> [Journalist name],
>
> I read your [date] piece on [topic]. I wanted to flag a factual inaccuracy that I'd appreciate you correcting.
>
> Your piece states [exact quote from article]. In fact, [accurate statement with source if applicable].
>
> I'm happy to speak with you directly to clarify. [Chapter Name] is committed to accurate public information about cannabis policy in [state].
>
> [Name, title, contact info]

---

### Scenario 4: Social Media Goes Wrong

**When it happens:** A post goes out that was wrong, poorly worded, or created a public backlash.

**Response steps:**
1. Delete the post immediately if it contains factual errors or is genuinely harmful
2. Do not delete if it is simply controversial but accurate — deleting looks worse
3. Post a correction or clarification within 2 hours if needed
4. Brief the board before the end of the day

**Correction post template:**
> An earlier post contained an error / did not represent [Chapter Name]'s position accurately. We've removed it.
>
> Our position on [topic]: [accurate one-sentence statement].
>
> We apologize for any confusion.

---

### Scenario 5: Internal Conflict Goes Public

**When it happens:** A board dispute, staff situation, or internal disagreement becomes visible externally — through a disgruntled member, a leaked email, or a public resignation.

**What NOT to do:** Address the internal situation publicly. Attack the individual publicly. Pretend nothing happened when it clearly did.

**Response steps:**
1. Do not respond to specific allegations publicly
2. Issue a brief statement that redirects to mission
3. Handle the internal situation internally

**Statement template:**
> [Chapter Name] is focused on our mission: advocacy for cannabis patients and reform in [state].
>
> We do not comment on internal organizational matters. Our work continues.
>
> [Website / contact for legitimate media inquiries]

---

## Crisis Preparation Checklist

Do these before any crisis happens:

- [ ] Designate one spokesperson (usually Executive Director) — only they speak publicly during a crisis
- [ ] Board has each other's phone numbers and can convene within 24 hours
- [ ] All social media accounts have at least 2 admin users (so access isn't lost if one person is involved in the crisis)
- [ ] Chapter has a media contact list — know who covers cannabis in your state
- [ ] All statement templates are in the chapter's Google Drive — accessible without hunting

---

## Media Protocol

**Who talks to media:** Executive Director only, unless specifically delegated
**Response time:** Acknowledge media inquiries within 2 hours, full response within 24 hours
**"No comment" rule:** Use the holding statement templates instead of "no comment" — it sounds better and says the same thing
**On background:** Never speak to a journalist "off the record" unless you are prepared for it to become on the record
