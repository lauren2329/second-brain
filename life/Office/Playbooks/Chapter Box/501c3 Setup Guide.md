## Why Every Chapter Needs a 501(c)(3) Arm

The NORML state chapter is a 501(c)(4) — built for advocacy. That's correct. But a 501(c)(4) alone locks the chapter out of:

- Google Ad Grants ($10,000/month in free advertising)
- Google Workspace for Nonprofits (free email, Drive, Meet, etc.)
- TechSoup (deep discounts on software — Microsoft, Adobe, Zoom, Salesforce)
- Tax-deductible donations (donors cannot write off gifts to a 501(c)(4))
- Most foundation grants
- Candid Seal of Transparency (which funders require)

The solution: a 501(c)(3) education and community arm operating alongside the 501(c)(4) chapter.

---

## Two Paths

### Option A — Partner with Common Good Network

[[Common Good Network 501c3]] is KYNORML's 501(c)(3) education arm. VMP can facilitate a formal partnership between a state chapter and CGN for educational programming purposes — allowing the chapter to access CGN's 501(c)(3) status for:

- Google Ad Grants (CGN applies, shares access)
- Google Workspace for Nonprofits
- Educational grant applications run through CGN
- Tax-deductible donation processing for educational activities

**How it works:** CGN acts as the chapter's educational programming partner. The chapter's advocacy work stays under its 501(c)(4). Educational content, wellness programs, and patient education run through CGN. Revenue from educational activities flows to CGN and is shared with the chapter per a program partnership agreement.

**Best for:** Chapters that want to move fast without forming a new entity. Gets you 501(c)(3) access within weeks rather than months.

**Contact VMP** to set up a CGN partnership agreement.

---

### Option B — Form Your Own 501(c)(3)

The chapter's education entity is independent — owned by the chapter, governed by the chapter's board (or a separate board), and operated alongside the 501(c)(4).

**Best for:** Chapters that want full control of their educational arm, want their own grant identity, or are large enough to justify a separate entity.

**Naming convention:** [State] Cannabis Education [Fund/Network/Alliance] or similar — distinct from the chapter name, clearly educational in mission.

**Timeline:** Filing takes 1–4 weeks. IRS determination letter takes 3–6 months. Google Ad Grants and most platforms accept a copy of the application plus EIN while you wait.

## Forming the 501(c)(3) — Step by Step

### Step 1 — File the Entity

Same as the 501(c)(4) process in [[Governance Box]], but with an educational mission statement.

- File Articles of Incorporation with your state
- Get an EIN at IRS.gov
- Mission statement example: "To provide public education on cannabis as a wellness resource, patient rights, and evidence-based cannabis policy."

### Step 2 — Apply for 501(c)(3) Status

**Form 1023-EZ** (for orgs projecting under $50k/year in the first 3 years):
- Online at pay.gov
- Filing fee: $275
- Processing time: 2–4 weeks

**Form 1023** (for larger orgs):
- More detailed — narrative descriptions of programs, financials
- Filing fee: $600
- Processing time: 3–6 months

**While waiting for determination:**
Most platforms (Google, TechSoup, Candid) accept your EIN + Form 1023/1023-EZ confirmation receipt as proof of pending 501(c)(3) status. Do not wait for the determination letter to start registering.

## Register on Every Platform — Do This Immediately After EIN

### 1. Candid (GuideStar) — candid.org

**What it is:** The national nonprofit database. Funders, grant committees, and major donors check Candid before giving. An unclaimed or incomplete profile signals an amateur organization.

**How to register:**
1. Go to candid.org → "Claim your nonprofit profile"
2. Search your EIN — your org will appear if the IRS has processed it
3. Create an account and claim the profile
4. Fill out every field completely

**The Seal of Transparency:**
Candid awards seals at four levels based on how much information you provide.

| Seal | What It Requires |
|---|---|
| **Bronze** | Basic organizational info — name, mission, contact |
| **Silver** | Above + program descriptions, leadership info |
| **Gold** | Above + financial data (990 or financials) |
| **Platinum** | Above + metrics reporting (outcomes, impact data) |

**Target: Gold seal minimum.** Most foundations require at least a Silver seal to be considered for grants. Gold is the standard that signals organizational health.

**Why it matters:** A funder searching your org name will see your Candid profile. A blank or Bronze profile next to a Gold-sealed org is a disqualifier.

---

### 2. TechSoup — techsoup.org

**What it is:** A nonprofit that provides technology products and discounts to qualifying organizations. Access requires 501(c)(3) verification.

**Products available (partial list):**

| Product | Retail Value | TechSoup Price |
|---|---|---|
| Microsoft 365 Nonprofit | $12.50/user/month | $0–$3/user/month |
| Adobe Creative Cloud | $80/month | ~$20/month |
| Zoom for Nonprofits | $16/month | Discounted |
| Salesforce Nonprofit | $300/user/month | Free (10 licenses) |
| DocuSign | $300/year | ~$36/year |
| Intuit QuickBooks | $80/month | $0 (donated) |

**How to register:**
1. Go to techsoup.org → "Register your organization"
2. Submit: EIN, 501(c)(3) determination letter (or application confirmation)
3. Pay eligibility fee: $0 for most nonprofits (small admin fee for some product categories)
4. Verification takes 5–10 business days
5. Once verified: browse and request donated/discounted software

**Do this immediately.** QuickBooks alone saves $80/month. Microsoft 365 saves $100+/month. Adobe saves $60/month. A chapter that registers on day one reduces its operating costs by $200–$300/month or more.

---

### 3. Google for Nonprofits — google.org/nonprofits

**What it unlocks:**
- Google Ad Grants ($10,000/month in free search ads)
- Google Workspace for Nonprofits (free Gmail, Drive, Meet, Docs, Sheets, etc.)
- YouTube Nonprofit Program
- Google Maps credit

**How to register:**
1. Go to google.org/nonprofits
2. Verify eligibility: must be a 501(c)(3), not a government entity, hospital, or school
3. Apply through Percent (Google's validation partner)
4. Submit: EIN, 501(c)(3) determination letter
5. Approval takes 2–14 business days

**After approval:**
- Activate Google Workspace for Nonprofits first — sets up the organizational email and Drive
- Then apply for Google Ad Grants separately within the Google for Nonprofits portal
- Ad Grants approval takes an additional 1–4 weeks
- See [[Track 3 - Google Ads Playbook]] for campaign setup after activation

---

### 4. Charity Navigator — charitynavigator.org

**What it is:** The most widely recognized independent nonprofit evaluator. Donors and major funders check Charity Navigator ratings before giving.

**How it works:**
- Charity Navigator automatically pulls data from IRS 990 filings
- Rates nonprofits on: finance, accountability, transparency, impact
- Organizations with no 990 on file receive no rating — which looks worse than a low rating

**What to do:**
1. File your first 990 on time — this is the foundation of your Charity Navigator profile
2. Once a rating appears, claim your profile at charitynavigator.org
3. Add narrative information: mission, programs, leadership
4. Address any concerns flagged in the rating

**Timeline:** Charity Navigator profiles appear after the first 990 is processed by the IRS — typically 6–12 months after filing.

---

### 5. IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search

**What it is:** The public IRS database where anyone can verify a nonprofit's tax-exempt status.

**What to do:** Nothing — the IRS populates this automatically once 501(c)(3) status is granted. But verify your listing is accurate after receiving your determination letter.

URL: apps.irs.gov/app/eos

---

### 6. State Charity Registration

Most states require nonprofits to register with the state attorney general's office before soliciting donations. Requirements vary by state.

**What to do:**
1. Search "[Your state] charity registration requirement"
2. Register with your state attorney general's charitable solicitations division
3. Renew annually — most states require annual renewal with financial reporting

Failure to register can result in fines and forced refund of donations.

- [ ] Claim Charity Navigator profile once it appears
