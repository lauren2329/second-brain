## Overview

A chapter without legal structure is a group of people with a name. This box makes it a real organization. VMP handles the filing research and setup guidance — the chapter provides the people and the signatures.

---

## Entity Structure

Most NORML state chapters operate as one of two entity types:

| Structure | Best For | Restrictions |
|---|---|---|
| **501(c)(4) Social Welfare** | Advocacy, lobbying, political activity | Cannot have donations be tax-deductible |
| **501(c)(3) Educational** | Education, community programming, grants | Cannot engage in substantial lobbying or political activity |

**Recommended:** 501(c)(4) for the chapter + 501(c)(3) for an education arm (like KYNORML + CGN). This gives the chapter full advocacy freedom AND access to grants, Google Ad Grants, and tax-deductible donations through the education entity.

## Board Structure

### Minimum Required Officers

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **President / Executive Director** | Leads the organization, primary spokesperson, signs contracts |
| **Vice President** | Supports the President, steps in when needed |
| **Secretary** | Meeting minutes, official records, correspondence |
| **Treasurer** | Financial oversight, signatures on accounts, budget reporting |

### Recommended Additional Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Board Member at Large (2–3)** | Voting members, committees, community representation |
| **Legal Advisor** | Cannabis-adjacent legal guidance, compliance review (can be advisory, non-voting) |

### Board Operating Rules

- Minimum 3 board members to have a valid quorum
- Board meets at minimum quarterly — monthly recommended
- Decisions require a majority vote of quorum
- No single person should control finances alone — require dual signatures on accounts over $[X]
- Term limits: 2-year terms, maximum 2 consecutive terms (prevents stagnation)

## Bylaws Template

Key sections every NORML chapter bylaws must include:

**Article I — Name and Purpose**
State the organization name, the state, and the mission (advocacy for cannabis reform in [state]).

**Article II — Membership**
Define who is a member, membership dues structure, member rights and responsibilities.

**Article III — Board of Directors**
Number of directors, election process, terms, removal procedures, vacancy filling.

**Article IV — Officers**
List of officers, how they are elected, duties of each, term lengths.

**Article V — Meetings**
Frequency of board meetings, quorum requirements, notice requirements, voting procedures.

**Article VI — Finances**
Fiscal year (recommend January 1 – December 31), banking authority, budget approval process, audit requirements.

**Article VII — Amendments**
How bylaws can be amended (recommend 2/3 board vote with 30 days notice).

**Article VIII — Dissolution**
What happens to assets if the chapter dissolves (must go to another nonprofit, not to individuals).

## IRS Compliance Checklist

### Annual Requirements

- [ ] **Form 990 filing** — due 4.5 months after fiscal year end (May 15 for calendar year orgs)
  - 990-N (e-Postcard): gross receipts under $50K
  - 990-EZ: gross receipts $50K–$200K
  - 990: gross receipts over $200K
- [ ] **State annual report** — varies by state, usually due on anniversary of incorporation
- [ ] **State charitable solicitation registration** — most states require this before fundraising
- [ ] **Board meeting minutes** — keep on file, at minimum one set per year
- [ ] **Conflict of interest policy** — board members must sign annually

### One-Time Setup Requirements

- [ ] File Articles of Incorporation with state
- [ ] Apply for EIN (Employer Identification Number) — free at IRS.gov
- [ ] Apply for 501(c)(4) status — Form 1024-A, $600 filing fee
- [ ] Register with state attorney general for charitable solicitations
- [ ] Open organizational bank account (requires EIN + Articles of Incorporation)

## Banking Setup

### Requirements to Open a Nonprofit Account

- EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- Articles of Incorporation
- Board resolution authorizing the account (1-page document, VMP provides template)
- Two authorized signatories (President + Treasurer recommended)

### Recommended Banks

- Local credit unions (lower fees, community relationships)
- Bank of America / Chase (nonprofit accounts, online tools)
- Avoid online-only banks for nonprofit accounts — some don't accept nonprofits

### Account Controls

- Require dual signatures for transactions over $[500–1,000]
- Treasurer reviews bank statements monthly
- Board reviews financials quarterly
- No personal funds mixed with organizational funds — ever
