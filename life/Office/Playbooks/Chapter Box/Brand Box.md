## Overview

A chapter that looks amateur gets treated like one — by legislators, sponsors, media, and potential members. Brand consistency signals that this organization is real, stable, and worth taking seriously.

This box gives every chapter a professional brand foundation without a designer or marketing budget.

---

## Brand Hierarchy

National NORML owns the master brand. State chapters are sub-brands.

| Element | Who Controls It |
|---|---|
| NORML name and logo | National NORML — chapters may use per guidelines |
| State chapter name | Chapter — e.g., "KYNORML," "Colorado NORML," "Texas NORML" |
| Chapter logo | Chapter — must work alongside national NORML brand |
| Chapter colors, fonts | Chapter — should complement, not clash with national |
| Chapter voice | Chapter — state-specific, but consistent with NORML's mission tone |

---

## Chapter Name Format

Standard format: **[STATE ABBREVIATION]NORML** or **[STATE NAME] NORML**
Examples: KYNORML, Colorado NORML, Ohio NORML

Use one format consistently everywhere — website, email, social, legal documents. Do not alternate.

---

## Logo Usage

### National NORML Logo

- Use exactly as provided — no stretching, recoloring, or modification
- Always use on a background where it is clearly readable
- Download from National NORML's media resources — do not recreate
- Credit: "In partnership with NORML" or "A NORML chapter"

### Chapter Logo

- Must include the chapter name
- Should incorporate the NORML visual identity (font style, color family)
- Create in Canva using national NORML's brand as a reference
- Produce in three formats: full color, white/reversed, black/grayscale
- Minimum sizes: no smaller than 1 inch wide in print, 100px wide on screen

---

## Colors and Fonts

### NORML National Colors (reference)
- Primary green: NORML's established green
- Use national NORML's published brand guide for exact hex codes

### Chapter Color Guidance
- Choose 2 primary colors (one should align with or complement NORML green)
- Choose 1 accent color
- Stick to these 3 colors across all materials — no rainbow chapters

### Fonts
- Choose 1 heading font, 1 body font — free options: Google Fonts
- Use the same fonts everywhere: social graphics, email, documents, print
- Never use more than 2 fonts in a single piece

---

## Voice and Tone

### What NORML Chapters Sound Like

| Quality | Means |
|---|---|
| Authoritative | We know cannabis law and policy — we cite facts, not feelings |
| Accessible | We speak to patients, not just advocates — no jargon without explanation |
| Urgent but not panicked | There is real work to do — we are calm and focused, not reactive |
| Local | We know our state — we name our legislators, our cities, our patients |

### What to Avoid

- Sounding like a drug culture page — NORML is a policy and patient advocacy organization
- Overpromising — do not announce wins before they happen
- Attacking individuals — attack policies, never people
- Casual spelling and grammar errors — they undermine credibility

## Social Media Policy

### Account Ownership

- All chapter social accounts must be owned by the organization, not an individual
- Admin access must be held by at least 2 board members at all times
- When a social media manager leaves, remove their access within 24 hours

### Posting Standards

- All posts reviewed by at least one board member before going live (can be the person posting, if they are a board member)
- No posts that could be interpreted as endorsing specific political candidates — NORML chapters cannot do this
- No posts that make legal claims about cannabis without citation
- No posts using language that could embarrass the chapter publicly

### Response Policy

- Respond to comments and DMs within 48 hours
- Do not engage with trolls or hostile accounts — block and move on
- Escalate any threat or harassment to the board immediately

### Crisis Social Protocol

If a major news event affects the chapter, do not post until a board member has reviewed and approved the message. See [[Crisis Communications Box]].

## Email Signature Standard

Every chapter email should use the same format:

---

**[Name]**
[Title] | [Chapter Name]
[Email] | [Phone if applicable]
[Website URL]

*Fighting for cannabis reform in [State] since [Year] | A NORML chapter*

---

## Co-Branding with National NORML

When producing materials that reference both the state chapter and national NORML:

- Chapter name/logo takes primary position (it's your materials)
- National NORML referenced as "A NORML chapter" or "In partnership with NORML"
- Never imply that national NORML is directly endorsing a specific state campaign unless they have explicitly done so in writing
- Donation links: always use chapter links in chapter materials — see [[National NORML Resources Integration]] for the donation distinction

## Canva Setup

VMP sets up a shared Canva workspace for the chapter with:
- Chapter logo uploaded
- Brand colors set
- Brand fonts set
- Template library: social posts (all sizes), email headers, event flyers, sponsorship deck, press release header

Once set up, anyone on the team can create on-brand materials without design experience.
