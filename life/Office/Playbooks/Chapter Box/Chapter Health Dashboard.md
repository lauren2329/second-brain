A chapter that doesn't measure doesn't improve. This dashboard tells you at a glance whether a chapter is growing, stable, or declining — before the problem becomes a crisis.

VMP configures this as a Google Sheet shared with the chapter board.

## Health Indicators

Use this at each board meeting. Quick visual on chapter health.

| Area | Green | Yellow | Red |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cash** | 3+ months reserve | 1–3 months reserve | Under 1 month reserve |
| **Email list** | Growing month over month | Flat | Shrinking |
| **Open rate** | ≥ 25% | 15–24% | Under 15% |
| **Membership** | Net positive each month | Flat | Losing members |
| **Revenue** | At or above budget | Within 15% of budget | More than 15% below budget |
| **Advocacy** | Active on 1+ legislative issues | Monitoring only | No legislative activity |
| **Board** | All seats filled, quorum at every meeting | 1 vacancy | Multiple vacancies or missing quorum |
| **Filing compliance** | All filings current | Filing due within 30 days | Filing overdue |

## Quarterly Board Review

Every quarter, the board reviews the full health picture — not just financials.

**Agenda item:** Chapter Health Review (30 minutes)

**Review:**
1. Health indicator summary — how many green / yellow / red?
2. Revenue vs. budget — on track or off? Why?
3. Membership trend — growing or shrinking? Why?
4. Top 3 advocacy wins this quarter
5. Top 3 challenges this quarter
6. One thing to fix in the next 90 days

## When to Sound the Alarm

These are the indicators that require immediate board action — not waiting for the next meeting.

- Cash drops below 1 month of operating expenses
- Email list declines for 3 consecutive months
- 990 filing is overdue
- A board officer position is vacant for more than 60 days
- Revenue is more than 25% below budget at any quarter
- Chapter is publicly named in litigation
- A key vendor or platform terminates the chapter's account
