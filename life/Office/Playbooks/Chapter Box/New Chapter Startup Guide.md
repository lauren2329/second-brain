## Overview

For chapters starting from zero — no entity, no list, no tools, no systems. This guide covers the first 90 days in order. Do these things in sequence. Do not skip ahead.

VMP handles the hard parts. This guide shows what the timeline looks like.

---

## Before Day 1 — Connect with National NORML

Before anything else, contact National NORML to register as a new chapter.

- National NORML maintains the chapter network
- They provide resources, graphics, content, and national campaign support
- Chapter affiliation is what gives you access to the NORML brand

## First 30 Days — Legal and Financial Foundation

Do not do anything public until the legal and financial foundation is in place.

### Week 1 — Entity

- [ ] Choose entity structure (501(c)(4) strongly recommended — see [[Governance Box]])
- [ ] Choose the chapter name (format: [STATE]NORML or [State] NORML)
- [ ] File Articles of Incorporation with your state
- [ ] Apply for EIN at IRS.gov (free, takes 10 minutes online)

*VMP assists with: entity structure guidance, Articles of Incorporation template, EIN application*

### Week 2 — Board

- [ ] Identify founding board members — minimum 4 (President, VP, Secretary, Treasurer)
- [ ] Hold founding board meeting
- [ ] Adopt bylaws (see [[Governance Box]] for template)
- [ ] Pass board resolution to open a bank account
- [ ] Sign conflict of interest policies

*VMP provides: bylaws template, board meeting minutes template, conflict of interest policy template*

### Week 3 — Banking and Compliance

- [ ] Open organizational bank account (bring EIN, Articles, board resolution)
- [ ] Apply for 501(c)(4) status — Form 1024-A (or proceed with just state incorporation first if budget is tight — 501(c)(4) status can take 3–6 months)
- [ ] Register for state charitable solicitation (check your state's requirements)

*VMP assists with: bank account setup guidance, 1024-A filing overview, state registration checklist*

### Week 4 — Apply for 501(c)(3) Education Arm (Optional but Recommended)

- [ ] Decide whether to form a 501(c)(3) education entity alongside the 501(c)(4) chapter
- [ ] If yes: file separate Articles of Incorporation for the education entity
- [ ] Apply for 501(c)(3) status — Form 1023-EZ (for smaller orgs) or 1023

*Why this matters: Google Workspace for Nonprofits, Google Ad Grants, and tax-deductible donations all require 501(c)(3). The chapter needs the education arm to access these.*

## Days 30–60 — Technology and Brand

The legal foundation is in place. Now build the tools.

### Technology Setup (VMP handles all of this)

- [ ] Google Workspace for Nonprofits application (via 501(c)(3) arm)
- [ ] Custom email domain setup (director@[chaptername].org, etc.)
- [ ] Google Drive folder structure built and shared with board
- [ ] Action Network account setup
- [ ] Zeffy account setup — donation page, membership tiers
- [ ] BillTrack50 account — state bill alerts configured
- [ ] Monday.com board built — follow-up tracker, operations board
- [ ] Social media accounts created: Facebook, Instagram — minimum
- [ ] Buffer or Meta Business Suite connected for scheduling

### Brand Setup (VMP assists)

- [ ] Canva workspace created with chapter brand colors, fonts, logo
- [ ] Template library loaded: social posts, event flyers, sponsorship deck
- [ ] Email signature standard set for all board members

### Google Ad Grants (if 501(c)(3) arm is approved)

- [ ] Apply for Google Ad Grants through Google for Nonprofits
- [ ] Wait for approval (typically 1–4 weeks)
- [ ] VMP activates once approved — campaigns built and launched

## Days 60–90 — Build the Audience

The tools are in place. Now find the people.

### Build the Initial Email List

- [ ] Import any existing contacts — past supporters, allies, partners
- [ ] Post sign-up link on all social channels
- [ ] Add sign-up link to the chapter website
- [ ] Reach out personally to cannabis-adjacent organizations in the state — ask them to share the chapter with their audiences
- [ ] List growth is the #1 priority for the first 90 days — without an audience, nothing else works

### First Email Send

- [ ] Once list reaches 100+ subscribers: send a launch announcement
- [ ] Subject: "We're here. Here's what [Chapter Name] is fighting for."
- [ ] Body: Who you are, what you're fighting for, what you need from supporters (join, donate, follow)
- [ ] Include: membership link (Zeffy), social links, donation link

### Social Media — First 30 Days of Posts

- [ ] Set up a 2-week batch using the national NORML content + chapter intro posts
- [ ] Post 1: Who we are — chapter mission and founding story
- [ ] Post 2: Why cannabis reform matters in [state] — local patient context
- [ ] Posts 3–14: Mix of national NORML content (localized) + chapter updates

### First Public Presence

- [ ] Attend at least one cannabis-adjacent public event in the state
- [ ] Make contact with 5 cannabis businesses — introduce the chapter, get their email
- [ ] Introduce the chapter to at least one sympathetic state legislator

## Common Mistakes New Chapters Make

**1. Going public before the legal foundation is in place**
Announcing yourself as an organization before you have an EIN and bank account creates problems. Set up the entity first.

**2. Building on a personal social account**
Chapter accounts must be organizational, not personal. Build it right the first time.

**3. Skipping the 501(c)(3) education arm**
Many chapters operate only as a 501(c)(4) and wonder why they can't access grants or Google Ad Grants. Set up the education arm early.

**4. One person doing everything**
Chapters built on one person collapse when that person burns out or leaves. Distribute responsibilities across the board from day one.

**5. Never asking for money**
New chapters feel awkward asking for donations before they've "earned it." The reality: supporters give to organizations that ask. Ask early and often.

**6. Ignoring the national NORML network**
National NORML has resources, content, and connections that chapters don't have to build themselves. Plug in immediately.

**7. Waiting for the right moment to do advocacy**
There is no right moment. Legislation moves fast. Chapters that are already in relationship with legislators when a bill drops are the ones who get a hearing.
