## The Choice: Direct Outreach

The free guide reaches chapter directors through direct personal outreach — not through National NORML, not through a download page, not through social media.

**Why:**
- No gatekeepers or dependencies — starts immediately
- Personal contact builds the relationship that eventually converts to a paid engagement
- NORML's chapter directory is publicly available — every chapter contact is findable
- A personal email from a peer (another chapter director with 8 years of experience) is far more likely to be read than a link from an organization
- National NORML partnership and the Chapter Summit are still being built — direct outreach has no prerequisites

**The secondary channels** (norml.org resources page, NORML social groups, Chapter Summit) amplify reach over time. Direct outreach is the engine that starts it.

---

## The Outreach List

Every active NORML state chapter is listed at norml.org/chapters.

**Build the list:**
1. Go to norml.org/chapters
2. For each state chapter: name, state, website, contact email or director name
3. Log in Google Sheets — columns: State, Chapter Name, Director Name, Email, Outreach Date, Response, Status
4. 50 states + DC = approximately 50–60 chapters total

**Finding director contact info when not listed:**
- Chapter website — "About" or "Contact" page
- LinkedIn search: "[State] NORML director"
- Facebook: most chapters have a public page with contact info
- Action Network: some chapters have public organizer profiles

## The Outreach Email

Personal. Short. Peer to peer. No pitch.

**Subject:** `Free chapter operations guide — from KYNORML`

**Body:**

> [Director name],
>
> I'm Lauren Bratcher, Executive Director of KYNORML. We've been running Kentucky's chapter for 8 years and KYCannaCon for 3.
>
> Over the past year I put together a complete operations guide for NORML state chapters — revenue systems, marketing, annual conference model, advocacy toolkit, governance, tech stack, all of it. We're sharing it free with any chapter that wants it.
>
> No catch. It's just the system that works for us, documented so other chapters don't have to build it from scratch.
>
> [Link to guide or attached PDF]
>
> If you ever want help executing any of it, that's what we do through Verdant Management Partners — but the guide is yours either way.
>
> Lauren Bratcher
> Executive Director, KYNORML
> [Email] | [Phone]

**Rules:**
- Never send more than 10 outreach emails per day — keeps it personal, avoids spam filters
- Personalize the opening line when you know anything about their chapter
- No attachments over 5MB — use a Google Drive link instead
- Log every send in the tracker immediately

---

## The Follow-Up

One follow-up only. Seven days after the first email.

**Subject:** `Re: Free chapter operations guide — from KYNORML`

**Body:**

> Following up in case my last note got buried.
>
> Happy to send the guide or answer any questions about how we built KYNORML's operation. No obligation.
>
> Lauren

If no response after the follow-up — move on. Do not send a third email.

---

## The Conversion Moment

When a director responds — whether to say thank you, ask a question, or say they're overwhelmed — that is the conversion opportunity.

**If they say thank you:**
> "Glad it's useful. If you ever want us to set any of it up for you, that's what Verdant Management Partners does — we handle the execution so you can focus on advocacy. Happy to talk through what that looks like for [state] if you're ever curious."

**If they say they're overwhelmed:**
> "That's exactly why we built the execution service. Most chapters have the passion but not the bandwidth to build all of this. We do the setup, you run the chapter. Want to jump on a call?"

**If they ask about execution:**
> Schedule a call. Send the [[VMP-Chapter Revenue Share Agreement]] after the call.

## Secondary Distribution Channels

These run in parallel — they amplify but don't replace direct outreach.

**norml.org resources page**
- Submit the Chapter Box guide to National NORML for listing on their resources page
- Frame it as a contribution to the chapter network, not a VMP promotion
- Contact: [National NORML chapter coordinator — add contact]
- Timeline: submit once direct outreach campaign is underway

**NORML social groups**
- Post in NORML-affiliated Facebook groups and communities
- Frame as: "KYNORML built a free operations guide for state chapters — sharing it here in case it's useful to anyone"
- Do not make it a VMP pitch in the post — the guide links to VMP naturally

**The National Chapter Summit pitch**
- The guide becomes Exhibit A in the VMP pitch to National NORML
- "We built this guide for state chapters. The Summit is how we deliver it at scale and build the chapter network around it."
- See [[National NORML Chapter Conference]]

| | | | | | | | | |

**Status codes:**
- `Sent` — email delivered, no response yet
- `Responded` — replied to email
- `Call Scheduled` — moving toward execution conversation
- `Active Client` — signed revenue share agreement
- `DIY` — using the guide, not engaging VMP
- `No Response` — followed up, no reply
- `Not Interested` — declined
