## Overview

Chapters that don't track money lose it. This box gives every chapter the same financial system — simple enough for a volunteer treasurer, rigorous enough to satisfy a funder.

---

## Bookkeeping Setup

### Recommended Tool: Wave (free)
Wave Accounting is free for nonprofits and covers everything a chapter needs:
- Income and expense tracking
- Invoicing (for vendor fees, sponsorships)
- Financial reports (P&L, balance sheet)
- Receipt scanning

**Alternative:** QuickBooks for Nonprofits (paid, more robust — recommended when revenue exceeds $50K/year)

### Chart of Accounts (Standard for NORML Chapters)

**Income**
- Membership dues
- Event revenue — tickets
- Event revenue — vendor fees
- Event revenue — sponsorships
- Donations — individual
- Donations — matching campaigns
- Merch sales
- Directory listing fees
- Grants
- Other income

**Expenses**
- Event production — venue
- Event production — AV / tech
- Event production — printing / signage
- Event production — speaker honorarium
- Marketing — paid (if any)
- Technology (tools, subscriptions)
- Director travel
- Administrative
- Legal / compliance
- Banking fees

## Expense and Reimbursement Policy

### What Requires Prior Approval

- Any single expense over $[250]
- Any new vendor relationship
- Any travel expense
- Any expense outside the approved budget

### Reimbursement Process

1. Submit receipt within 30 days of expense
2. Include: receipt, amount, purpose, event/project it relates to
3. Treasurer approves and processes within 14 days
4. Reimbursed via check or direct deposit — never cash

### What Is Never Reimbursable

- Personal expenses of any kind
- Alcohol (unless explicitly pre-approved for a specific event)
- Expenses without receipts
- Expenses that weren't pre-approved when required

## Financial Controls

| Control | Why |
|---|---|
| Dual signatures on accounts over $[500] | Prevents single-person fraud |
| Treasurer ≠ President | Separation of duties |
| Board reviews financials quarterly | Oversight |
| Annual 990 filing | IRS compliance |
| No commingling of personal and chapter funds | Legal protection |
| Keep 3 months of operating expenses in reserve | Survival |

---

## Reserve Fund Goal

Every chapter should maintain a reserve equal to 3 months of operating expenses.

If monthly operating expenses are $2,000 → reserve target is $6,000.

Reserve sits in a separate savings account. Do not touch it except for genuine emergency.
