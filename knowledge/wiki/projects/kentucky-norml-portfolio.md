## 1. Kentucky Cannabis Conference

**Status:** Active Development — Year 4 Planning (2027)

This is currently the organization's largest annual event and Kentucky's annual cannabis ecosystem gathering.

### Conference Mission

Unlike a trade association conference, the Kentucky Cannabis Conference exists to bring together:
- Consumers
- Patients
- Industry
- Regulators
- Healthcare professionals
- Elected officials
- Community leaders

### Event History

**Year 1 (2024) — Proof of Concept**
- Location: Frankfort
- Attendance: 145+ paid attendees
- Exhibitors/Presenters: 26
- Established proof of concept with legislators and regulators in attendance

**Year 2 (2025) — Educational Expansion**
- Continued patient, advocate, and policy focus
- Expanded educational programming

**Year 3 (2026) — Growth & Challenges**
- Location: Kentucky International Convention Center (KICC), Louisville
- Dates: March 20–21, 2026
- Included expo floor, speakers, media access, networking
- **Identified challenges:**
  - Too many sessions competing with each other
  - Limited organizer visibility
  - Insufficient "anchor moments"
  - Attendance and flow issues

### Strategic Evolution

**Moving from:** Conference → **To:** Annual Industry Gathering

The major shift for Year 4 is creating an event people plan their calendars around rather than another educational conference.

**Key Emphases:**
- More time together
- Less room hopping
- Shared experiences
- Meals as programming
- Relationship building
- Fewer concurrent sessions
- Stronger anchor events

## Consumer Choice Awards

**Strategic Importance:** May become Kentucky NORML's most valuable brand asset.

### Purpose & Differentiation

**Most cannabis awards are:**
- Pay-to-play
- Judge-selected
- Industry-selected

**Consumer Choice Awards:**
- **Chosen by Kentucky consumers**
- Aligns directly with Kentucky NORML's consumer protection mission
- Drives directory engagement
- Creates annual consumer anticipation
- Gives businesses reasons to participate beyond panels

### Strategic Benefits

**Drives Directory Traffic**
- Leverages established WordPress directory
- Instead of "Visit our directory," consumers are encouraged to: find businesses, review businesses, vote for businesses

**Creates Annual Anticipation**
- People will ask "Who won this year?" every year

**Gives Businesses a Reason to Participate**
- Dispensary owner motivation: "Best Dispensary in Kentucky" award, not a panel

**Creates Sponsorship Opportunities**
- Awards sponsor
- Voting sponsor
- Category sponsor
- Reception sponsor
- Directory sponsor

### Voting Model

**Phase 1: Nominations**
- Open statewide
- Consumers nominate

**Phase 2: Verification**
- Kentucky NORML verifies nominees

**Phase 3: Voting**
- Public voting
- Directory-driven

**Phase 4: Awards**
- Presented during conference

### Potential Award Categories

**Consumer Experience**
- Best Dispensary
- Best Customer Service
- Best New Dispensary
- Best Patient Experience

**Product Categories** (as market matures)
- Best Flower
- Best Vape
- Best Edible
- Best Concentrate
- Best Topical

**Industry Categories**
- Best Cultivator
- Best Processor
- Best Brand
- Best Packaging

**Community Categories** (align with NORML mission)
- Consumer Advocate of the Year
- Community Impact Award
- Patient Champion Award
- Educator of the Year

### What the Awards Should NOT Become

Avoid:
- Black tie gala
- Expensive banquet
- Industry insider popularity contest
- Pay-to-win award program

These alternatives already exist elsewhere.

### Vision of Success (Years 5–6)

- Consumers know: "Voting opens every January"
- Businesses know: "We need to campaign for votes"
- Sponsors know: "This is where consumer attention is"
- Kentucky NORML owns:
  - The conference
  - The directory traffic
  - The annual awards program
  - The consumer voice

This combination creates a defensible ecosystem that is much harder for competing events or organizations to replicate than simply hosting another cannabis conference.
