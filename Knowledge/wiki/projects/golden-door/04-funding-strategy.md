# Golden Door Funding Strategy

## Objective
Open Golden Door with enough unrestricted cash to survive delays, preserve compliance, and avoid using restricted credits as if they were cash.

## Confirmed Sources
- Harry: $50,000 startup cash.
- Jeremy: $35,000 startup cash.
- Total opening liquidity: $85,000.
- Arrow: $50,000 cash incentive expected about 45 days after opening.
- Arrow: $40,000 restricted credit, plus $10,000 in years 2 and 3.

## Funding Rules
- Restricted credits cannot fund rent, payroll, debt service, taxes, or owner draws.
- Every loan, advance, reimbursement, or contribution must be documented before money moves.
- CGN and VMP funds must remain separate.
- Personal expenses may not be paid from CGN accounts.
- Related-party obligations require board approval and written terms.

## Priority Uses of Opening Cash
1. Occupancy and required deposits.
2. Licensing, insurance, legal, and compliance.
3. Equipment and launch-critical buildout.
4. Cash till and operating cash controls.
5. Minimum staffing and training.
6. Opening inventory and supplies.
7. Emergency reserve.

## Reserve Target
Preserve, at minimum:
- Three months of fixed occupancy costs.
- One month of staffing and contractor costs.
- Insurance deductibles and emergency repairs.
- A regulatory-delay cushion.

## Debt Strategy
Jeremy obligation:
- Principal tracked: $60,000.
- Repayment: $75,000 over 24 months.
- Monthly payment: $3,125.

No additional debt should be accepted unless the monthly cash-flow model shows coverage after rent, staffing, state fees, vendor fees, and reserves.

## Future Funding Options
- Board-approved donor campaign.
- Program sponsorships.
- Grants that fit CGN's charitable purpose.
- VMP business credit for VMP expenses only.
- Equipment financing where the asset and repayment source are clear.

## Decisions Still Required
- Final treatment of Harry's funding.
- Exact Arrow contract and payment conditions.
- Final startup uses budget.
- Minimum reserve amount in dollars.
- Whether any pre-opening expenses should be reimbursed and by which entity.
