# Golden Door Risk Register

## Purpose
This register identifies the principal risks that could delay opening, threaten cash flow, create compliance exposure, or damage Golden Door and Common Good Network. It is a management tool, not a substitute for legal, accounting, insurance, or regulatory advice.

## Rating Scale
- **Likelihood:** Low, Medium, High
- **Impact:** Low, Medium, High, Critical
- **Priority:** Immediate, High, Moderate, Monitor

## Opening-Day Stop Conditions
Golden Door should not open unless all of the following are confirmed:

- Required license and regulatory approvals are active.
- Qualified gaming chairperson coverage is scheduled.
- Insurance is bound and reviewed.
- The payout reserve is funded and physically controlled.
- Cash-handling, opening, closing, incident, and emergency procedures are trained.
- Bank accounts and books are separated by entity.
- Machines, security, accessibility, fire safety, internet, and payment systems are operational.
- Required contracts, board approvals, and related-party disclosures are documented.
- A minimum operating reserve remains after final pre-opening spending.

## Weekly Launch Review
Until 90 days after opening, management should review:

1. Cash balance and 13-week forecast.
2. Payout reserve balance and cash variances.
3. Revenue by day, hour, and machine.
4. Staffing and chairperson coverage.
5. Incidents, complaints, refunds, and unusual payouts.
6. Regulatory deadlines and open compliance items.
7. Vendor downtime and unresolved facilities issues.
8. Marketing source data and repeat-visit rate.
9. Debt obligations and related-party payments.
10. New risks, changed ratings, and required corrective actions.

## Escalation Rules
- **Immediate:** Operations pause or board/executive decision required now.
- **High:** Corrective action plan within seven days.
- **Moderate:** Assigned owner and monthly review.
- **Monitor:** Retain on register and reassess quarterly.

## Document Control
Every risk must have:

- One accountable owner.
- A current status.
- A control or mitigation plan.
- A target completion date when corrective action is required.
- Supporting documentation linked from the project repository.

This register should be updated whenever a material contract, funding source, operational process, regulatory requirement, or location condition changes.
