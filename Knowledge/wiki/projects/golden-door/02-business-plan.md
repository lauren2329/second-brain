# Golden Door Business Plan

## Purpose
Golden Door is a charitable social gaming lounge and community gathering program operated by Common Good Network Inc. (CGN) in Bowling Green, Kentucky.

## Operating Structure
- CGN owns and operates the charitable gaming activity.
- Verdant Ventures LLC dba Verdant Management Partners (VMP) provides management and administrative services under written agreements.
- Gaming proceeds remain with CGN and are accounted for separately.
- Related-party transactions require written terms, board approval, and clear documentation.

## Concept
Golden Door will pair a refined, salon-like environment with regulated charitable gaming. The intended customer experience is calm, clean, welcoming, adult-focused, and service-oriented rather than loud or casino-like.

## Initial Footprint
- Proposed location: 105 Creekwood Ave, Bowling Green, Kentucky.
- Approximate building size: 1,700 square feet.
- Initial machine count assumption: 35 machines.
- Proposed public hours:
  - Monday: closed
  - Tuesday-Thursday: 11:00 a.m.-7:00 p.m.
  - Friday-Saturday: 11:00 a.m.-8:00 p.m.
  - Sunday: 1:00 p.m.-5:00 p.m.
- Staffing coverage must include one hour before opening and one hour after closing.

## Revenue Model
Primary revenue is expected from charitable gaming activity, subject to state fees, vendor terms, and regulatory restrictions. Secondary revenue may include compliant retail, events, memberships, sponsorships, and community programming only where legally and operationally appropriate.

## Customer Segments
- Adults seeking a social gaming experience.
- Existing supporters of CGN and partner organizations.
- Community members drawn to a safer, more comfortable venue.
- Women and customers who prefer a hospitality-led environment.

## Competitive Position
Golden Door will compete through atmosphere, hospitality, trust, cleanliness, convenience, responsible operations, and community connection rather than through a race to offer the loudest or most aggressive gaming environment.

## Management Priorities
1. Obtain and maintain all required approvals.
2. Protect CGN's charitable assets.
3. Open with sufficient cash reserves.
4. Build repeat traffic before expanding programs.
5. Standardize operations through documented systems and SOPs.

## Current Decision Rules
- Do not add new business lines before the core lounge is stable.
- Do not commingle gaming funds with VMP or personal accounts.
- Do not treat restricted Arrow credits as cash.
- Do not make undocumented payments to insiders.
- Do not rely on verbal financing terms.

## Open Items
- Final lease terms.
- Final licensing and regulatory confirmation.
- Final Arrow contract review.
- Final staffing roster and background-check eligibility.
- Final floor plan and machine placement.
- Final insurance package.
- Final opening budget and contingency reserve.
