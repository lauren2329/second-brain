# Golden Door Gift Shop / Wellness Market Model

## Core Idea

Golden Door can operate more like a small community entertainment lounge than a plain gaming room. The practical analogy is **Chuck E. Cheese**, but adapted for adults, charitable gaming, wellness, and nonprofit fundraising.

The space can include:

- A charitable gaming area
- A wellness-focused retail/gift shop area
- Food, drinks, or concessions
- A front welcome/check-in counter
- Lounge seating
- Educational or nonprofit resource displays

The goal is to make Golden Door feel like a **community wellness lounge with charitable gaming**, not a bare-bones gambling room.

## Working Name

Avoid calling it only a "gift shop" in public materials. Better names:

- Golden Door Market
- Golden Door Mercantile
- Wellness Market
- Community Market
- Curated Wellness Boutique
- Resource Shop

Recommended public-facing language:

> Golden Door Market is a curated wellness and community retail area featuring local goods, self-care products, educational resources, nonprofit merchandise, and mission-aligned gifts.

## Regulatory Read

Kentucky charitable gaming regulations do not appear to prohibit a licensed charitable gaming facility from having other business activity inside the same facility.

The strongest regulatory hook is **820 KAR 1:005, Section 5(3)**. It says that a licensed gaming facility must maintain a separate bank account for the licensed gaming facility operation, and if separate businesses are operated out of the licensed gaming facility, including a check cashing service or concession stand, each business must have a separate bank account.

Source: Kentucky Legislative Research Commission, 820 KAR 1:005, Charitable gaming licenses and exemptions:  
https://apps.legislature.ky.gov/law/kar/titles/820/001/005/

This matters because the regulation expressly contemplates separate businesses operating out of a licensed charitable gaming facility. It does not list every possible business type. It gives examples, including check cashing and concession stands.

## Compliance Position

The safest position is:

> A wellness/gift shop may operate inside or alongside the Golden Door charitable gaming facility if it is treated as a separate non-gaming business activity with separate accounting, separate banking, separate inventory records, and no commingling with charitable gaming proceeds.

This is not a final legal opinion. It is a planning position to take to the attorney and/or Kentucky charitable gaming regulator before launch.

## Required Separation

Golden Door should keep clean separation between:

### 1. Charitable Gaming Activity

Includes:

- Charitable gaming receipts
- Prize payouts
- Gaming supplies
- Gaming reports
- Gaming bank account
- Gaming volunteers/chairpersons
- Required charitable gaming records

### 2. Retail / Wellness Market Activity

Includes:

- Product inventory
- Retail sales
- Sales tax records
- Cost of goods sold
- Retail merchant account
- Retail bank account
- Vendor payments
- Retail payroll or contractor payments, if any

### 3. Concessions / Drinks / Food, if used

May be its own separate business line or part of the retail operation, depending on final structure.

Includes:

- Hemp drinks, if permitted
- Coffee, tea, bottled drinks
- Packaged snacks
- Food permits, if applicable
- Separate tracking of taxable sales

## Suggested Operating Structure

### Option A: CGN Operates Everything Separately

Common Good Network operates:

- Licensed charitable gaming activity
- Retail/wellness market
- Concessions

This can work, but the accounting burden is higher because CGN must prove that gaming funds and retail funds are not commingled.

### Option B: VMP Operates the Retail/Market Side

VMP Management or another aligned LLC operates the retail/wellness market under a written agreement.

CGN operates or sponsors the charitable gaming activity.

This is cleaner if:

- VMP pays its own retail expenses
- VMP keeps its own bank account
- VMP owns or manages the retail inventory
- Any rent, service fee, management fee, or revenue share is documented in writing
- The agreement is reviewed for charitable gaming compliance

This may be the preferred model because it separates the commercial retail function from the charitable gaming funds.

## Money Flow Concept

```text
Customer enters Golden Door
│
├── Charitable gaming play
│   ├── Gaming revenue deposited to charitable gaming account
│   ├── Prizes paid according to gaming rules
│   ├── Gaming expenses paid only as permitted
│   └── Net charitable proceeds retained by CGN / used for mission
│
├── Retail / wellness market purchase
│   ├── Retail revenue deposited to retail account
│   ├── Inventory and vendor costs paid from retail account
│   ├── Sales tax tracked and remitted
│   └── Retail profit retained by operator or transferred only under compliant agreement
│
└── Concession / beverage purchase
    ├── Revenue deposited to retail or concession account
    ├── Cost of goods paid from retail/concession account
    └── Permits, taxes, and vendor records maintained separately
```

## Product Categories That Fit the Brand

Recommended categories:

- Potency 710 skincare
- Hemp beverages, if legally permitted and approved by lease/regulators
- Herbal teas
- Local honey
- Journals
- Candles
- Local pottery or artisan goods
- Wellness books
- Self-care tools
- CGN merchandise
- Golden Door merchandise
- Partner nonprofit merchandise
- Educational kits or printed guides
- Small seasonal gifts
- Greeting cards
- Community resource materials

## Product Categories to Avoid or Review Carefully

Avoid unless reviewed by counsel/regulator:

- Anything that looks like a gambling incentive or gambling prize substitute
- Gaming supplies
- Products that are given only to players based on gaming spend
- Sweepstakes-style promotions
- Alcohol, unless a separate licensing plan exists
- Age-restricted products without a strict compliance plan
- Anything paid for directly from charitable gaming proceeds without approval

## Customer Experience Model

The customer experience should feel like:

1. Walk into a welcoming front area.
2. See Golden Door Market, wellness items, local goods, and nonprofit information.
3. Check in or ask questions at the welcome desk.
4. Move into the charitable gaming area if they choose to play.
5. Buy drinks, skincare, gifts, or merch whether or not they play.

This gives Golden Door multiple reasons for people to enter:

- Gaming
- Shopping
- Community connection
- Wellness education
- Events
- Supporting nonprofit work

## Why This Strengthens Golden Door

A retail/wellness market helps Golden Door by:

- Making the location feel less like a gambling-only room
- Supporting the wellness-forward brand
- Creating non-gaming revenue
- Giving non-players a reason to visit
- Creating space for local makers and partner nonprofits
- Making advertising more flexible
- Supporting community credibility

## Advertising Position

Use language like:

> Golden Door is a community gaming and wellness lounge featuring charitable gaming, local goods, wellness products, nonprofit resources, and mission-aligned events.

Avoid language that suggests retail purchases improve gaming odds, earn gaming credits, or are tied to charitable gaming participation.

## Open Legal / Compliance Questions

Before launch, confirm:

- Whether the retail operation should be owned by CGN, VMP, or another LLC
- Whether the lease allows retail, concessions, hemp beverages, and charitable gaming
- Whether hemp beverages are allowed on-site under local and state rules
- Whether retail employees may also perform any gaming-adjacent duties
- Whether shared staff must clock into separate roles/entities
- Whether any revenue share between retail and CGN creates charitable gaming concerns
- Whether retail inventory can ever be purchased with CGN non-gaming funds
- Whether charitable gaming proceeds may be used for shared rent or utilities when retail also occupies the facility
- Whether signage needs to separate gaming activity from retail activity

## Practical Recommendation

Proceed with the Golden Door Market concept, but structure it as a separate business activity from charitable gaming.

Best working model:

- CGN handles charitable gaming compliance and mission use of net gaming proceeds.
- VMP or a separate operator handles Golden Door Market retail operations.
- Each revenue stream has its own bank account, bookkeeping, receipts, reporting, and written agreements.
- The attorney reviews all agreements before money moves.

## Bottom Line

A Chuck E. Cheese-style model is workable as a concept: one destination with multiple activity zones and revenue streams.

For Golden Door, the adult version is:

> Charitable gaming + wellness market + community lounge + nonprofit resource hub.

The compliance rule is simple but strict:

> Keep gaming money, retail money, concession money, staff duties, records, and agreements cleanly separated.
