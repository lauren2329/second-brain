# VMP Funding and Credit Strategy

## Current Direction

Verdant Ventures LLC d/b/a Verdant Management Partners (VMP) is the primary for-profit borrowing and credit-building entity.

Verdant Management Partners is not a separate company. All bank accounts, loans, credit applications, tax filings, contracts, and lender documents must use **Verdant Ventures LLC**, with **d/b/a Verdant Management Partners** added where appropriate.

Common Good Network Inc. (CGN) is a separate nonprofit. CGN owns and operates The Golden Door and retains all charitable gaming authority, licenses, revenue, supervision, cash accountability, and regulatory obligations.

## SBA Loan Reality

Verdant Ventures LLC does **not** have to be in business for three years before applying for an SBA-backed loan.

However, lenders generally prefer established operating history because they underwrite repayment ability, not merely entity age. A newer business can still qualify, but the file must rely more heavily on:

- Personal credit and guarantees
- Relevant management experience
- Written contracts and recurring revenue
- Accurate financial projections
- Owner investment or other available capital
- Clean banking and accounting records
- A credible repayment source

For a newer VMP application, personal credit will usually matter more than an undeveloped business credit score. Business credit becomes more useful as Verdant builds trade history, deposits, contracts, and financial statements.

## Recommended Funding Sequence

### 1. Make Verdant lender-ready

- Keep the legal name, EIN, address, phone, email, website, Secretary of State records, bank records, SAM registration, insurance, and vendor accounts consistent.
- Operate under one legal identity: Verdant Ventures LLC d/b/a Verdant Management Partners.
- Maintain business checking and accounting in QuickBooks.
- Use Ramp and other business financial products deliberately, without opening unnecessary accounts.
- Keep personal, nonprofit, gaming, and VMP money fully separated.

### 2. Document real revenue

- Execute written agreements with Kentucky NORML and CGN.
- Invoice every client consistently.
- Record all payments through the business bank account and QuickBooks.
- Preserve scopes of work, invoices, receipts, deliverables, and approval records.
- Build recurring management revenue rather than relying only on one-time projects.

### 3. Build business credit conservatively

- Verify the business files with Dun & Bradstreet, Experian Business, and Equifax Business.
- Use only vendors or accounts that serve a real business purpose.
- Confirm reporting before relying on a vendor as a credit-building account.
- Pay all accounts on time or early.
- Avoid spending money solely to manufacture tradelines.
- Track limits, balances, due dates, reporting bureaus, and personal-guarantee status.

### 4. Repair and protect personal credit

Because an SBA startup or newer-business loan will likely require personal guarantees, Lauren and Matthew's personal credit remains a major underwriting factor.

Priority actions:

- Pull all three personal credit reports from AnnualCreditReport.com.
- Verify balances, payment history, ownership, dates, and collection reporting.
- Dispute only inaccurate or unverifiable information.
- Do not file false or mass disputes.
- Keep every open account current through automatic minimum payments.
- Reduce revolving utilization before applying for financing.
- Avoid unnecessary hard inquiries and new consumer accounts.
- Keep older positive accounts open unless there is a strong reason to close them.
- Continue using the father's Wells Fargo authorized-user account only while it remains low-balance and perfectly paid.

Previously resolved personal debts include LVNV/Resurgent, Midland, and a judgment. These should be verified as accurately updated across all three credit bureaus.

## Financing Targets

The immediate goal is not to apply blindly for the largest SBA loan available. The goal is to build a file that can be presented to several funding sources.

Likely sequence:

1. Private startup capital already identified for the Golden Door launch
2. Vendor incentives and credits belonging to CGN
3. Business charge products and secured business credit used for ordinary VMP expenses
4. Community bank, credit union, or CDFI relationship financing
5. SBA Microloan or smaller working-capital product if appropriate
6. SBA 7(a) financing after VMP has stronger deposits, executed contracts, and reliable repayment history

A 504 loan is not the current primary target because the present plan is focused on startup and working capital rather than owner-occupied commercial real estate or major fixed equipment.

## Non-Negotiable Compliance Rules

- VMP may not claim ownership of CGN's gaming contract, gaming revenue, charitable gaming license, or Arrow incentives.
- CGN may pay VMP only for legitimate, reasonable, documented non-gaming services or approved reimbursements.
- Related-party agreements require disclosure, disinterested board approval where required, and clean minutes.
- Gaming funds may not be treated as ordinary VMP revenue unless lawfully paid under an approved agreement.
- Loan proceeds must be used only for the stated business purpose.
- All lender applications must be truthful and must use the correct legal entity.
