# Family Financial Strategy

## Purpose

This strategy explains how we move our immediate household from survival mode into stability, then use compliant business revenue to create long-term security and opportunity for the extended family.

The goal is not to hide income, misuse nonprofit resources, or create private benefit from charitable funds. The goal is to build a lawful family-enterprise system with clean books, accurate reporting, properly documented compensation, benefits-aware planning, and long-term asset protection.

## Core Strategy

The strategy has two layers:

1. Stabilize the immediate household first.
2. Build a compliant extended-family enterprise system over time.

The first revenue engines are connected to Common Good Network, the charitable gaming lounge, VMP management services, and related education or wellness programming. These entities must remain legally separate, with each entity only paying for the activities, wages, contracts, and expenses it is legally allowed to cover.

## Immediate Family Strategy

The immediate family strategy is to create stable household income while preserving access to lawful benefits, improving credit, and building basic security.

### Goals

- Keep housing stable.
- Cover food, utilities, transportation, and medical needs.
- Create reliable earned income through real documented work.
- Preserve public benefits only where legally eligible.
- Build personal credit for each adult.
- Separate personal money from business and nonprofit money.
- Use professional guidance for taxes, benefits, payroll, disability planning, and trust planning.

### Income Plan

Family members may be paid for legitimate work that supports the business or nonprofit mission, but every paid role must be real, documented, and reasonable.

Possible work areas include:

- Administration
- Operations
- Food and beverage planning
- Stocking and inventory
- Janitorial work
- Maintenance and handyman support
- Transportation coordination
- Event support
- Remote administrative work
- Benefits navigation or community resource work, if tied to the mission

Each role should have:

- A written job description
- Clear supervisor or reporting structure
- Hour tracking or deliverable tracking
- Reasonable pay rate
- Proper payroll or contractor treatment
- Correct entity paying the worker
- No payment from restricted funds unless legally permitted

## Benefits-Aware Planning

We will not frame the strategy as staying poor on paper. That language creates legal and ethical risk.

The correct framing is benefits-aware planning.

Benefits-aware planning means:

- Reporting income accurately.
- Reporting household changes accurately.
- Understanding income and asset limits before making changes.
- Avoiding accidental over-income when benefits are still needed.
- Using lawful deductions, business structures, trusts, ABLE accounts, or special needs planning where appropriate.
- Keeping business assets and personal assets clearly separated.
- Moving from benefit dependence to financial stability in a controlled way.

A clean version of the principle is:

> We will use legal benefits planning, accurate income reporting, clean business accounting, and appropriate trust structures so family members can work, build credit, access education, preserve disability-related supports where eligible, and gradually move from public-benefit dependence into long-term financial stability.

## Business Expense Rule

Businesses may pay for legitimate business expenses. Personal expenses must not be disguised as business expenses.

Potential business expenses may include:

- Work vehicles
- Work phone plans
- Insurance
- Office supplies
- Software
- Work clothing or uniforms
- Facility rent
- Marketing
- Staff wages
- Contractor payments
- Professional services
- Equipment and maintenance

The test is simple:

> If the expense exists because of the business and is properly documented, the business may be able to pay it. If the expense is personal, it must stay personal.

## Extended Family Strategy

The extended family strategy is to use the first successful revenue engine as a foundation for broader family stability.

The goal is to create real work, real businesses, and real assets, not informal cash flow or undocumented support.

### Long-Term Opportunities

Possible extended-family opportunities include:

- Food and beverage service
- Catering or concessions
- Janitorial services
- Maintenance and handyman services
- Plumbing or trades businesses
- Transportation support
- Event staffing
- Administrative support
- Education and wellness programming
- Property maintenance or future property development

Each opportunity should be structured as either:

- A paid employee role
- A properly documented contractor role
- A vendor agreement
- A separate family-owned business
- A nonprofit volunteer role, if unpaid and compliant

## Entity Separation

The family strategy depends on keeping each bucket clean.

| Entity or bucket | Purpose | Can do | Cannot do |
|---|---|---|---|
| Common Good Network | Nonprofit mission, charitable programming, charitable gaming compliance | Run mission programs, manage compliant charitable activity, pay allowed expenses | Privately enrich family members or pay unrelated personal expenses |
| VMP or management LLC | Operations, consulting, administration, management services | Charge reasonable fees for real services and pay workers for real work | Improperly control restricted nonprofit or gaming funds |
| Family trust or future trust structure | Long-term asset protection and planning | Hold assets when properly created and managed | Hide assets or misrepresent eligibility for benefits |
| Personal household | Living expenses, wages, benefits, credit-building | Receive wages and lawful support | Mix personal spending with nonprofit or business accounts |
| Future operating businesses | Trades, food service, events, property, services | Create jobs, contracts, assets, and independent income | Use nonprofit resources as private capital without legal structure |

## Money Flow Principle

Money must flow through the entity that legally matches the activity.

- Nonprofit money serves the nonprofit mission.
- Gaming money follows charitable gaming law.
- LLC money pays for real services and business expenses.
- Family wages are paid for real work.
- Benefits are reported accurately.
- Trusts are used for lawful planning and protection, not concealment.

## Practical Build Order

1. Stabilize household income.
2. Keep benefits and reporting accurate.
3. Set up payroll, contractor agreements, and bookkeeping correctly.
4. Build business revenue through the gaming lounge and related services.
5. Pay family members for real work where legally allowed.
6. Repair and build personal credit.
7. Build business credit and cash reserves.
8. Add work vehicles, equipment, phones, and tools only when tied to legitimate business use.
9. Establish trusts or asset-protection structures with legal guidance.
10. Help extended family members spin up compliant businesses or roles.

## Plain-English Summary

For the immediate family, the strategy is to stabilize the household through lawful wages, benefits-aware planning, clean accounting, credit repair, and separation between personal, business, and nonprofit money.

For the extended family, the strategy is to build a family-enterprise platform: jobs first, then clean contracts, then business credit, then vehicles and equipment, then trusts and assets, then spin-off businesses for relatives.

The master rule is:

> Do not hide money. Structure it correctly.
