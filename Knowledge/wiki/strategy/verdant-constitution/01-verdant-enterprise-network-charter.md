# Verdant Enterprise Network Charter

## Purpose

The Verdant Enterprise Network exists to connect mission-driven nonprofits, socially responsible businesses, and independent service providers through a shared framework for management, administration, compliance coordination, economic opportunity, and community impact.

This network is not a single legal entity. It is a collaborative enterprise ecosystem. Each participating organization remains legally and financially independent unless a formal ownership, contract, employment, vendor, or partnership agreement says otherwise.

## Mission

To strengthen organizations and communities by providing professional management infrastructure while helping individuals, families, and small businesses build sustainable income, credible records, and long-term independence.

## Vision

Verdant will become a trusted enterprise network where nonprofit organizations, for-profit businesses, and independent service providers can work together without losing their separate legal identities, missions, or financial boundaries.

The goal is to create a structure where:

- Mission organizations can operate more professionally.
- For-profit businesses can serve meaningful work.
- Independent providers can build legitimate business records.
- Families can move from instability to capacity.
- Community benefit and economic opportunity reinforce each other.

## Core Philosophy

Verdant succeeds when its partners succeed.

The network is not designed to centralize power. It is designed to cultivate capable organizations, independent entrepreneurs, stable families, and stronger communities through ethical collaboration.

## Guiding Principles

1. Mission before ego.
2. Compliance before convenience.
3. Real services deserve real compensation.
4. Public benefit and private opportunity must stay legally distinct.
5. Transparency builds trust.
6. Clean records protect everyone.
7. Every participant should leave stronger than when they joined.
8. Independent businesses should be encouraged to grow beyond the network.
9. Financial success should increase community impact, not replace it.
10. No entity should be used as a shell, pass-through, or private piggy bank.

## Network Participants

Participants may include:

- Verdant Management Partners
- Verdant Ventures LLC
- Common Good Network
- Golden Door
- Portal to Wellness
- Wonderland Wellness World
- Potent C710
- Kentucky NORML
- Tennessee NORML
- Future nonprofit clients
- Future for-profit clients
- Future independent service providers
- Future strategic partners

Participation does not automatically create ownership, employment, agency, partnership, fiduciary authority, or control.

## Role of Verdant Management Partners

Verdant Management Partners serves as the management and administrative hub for the network.

VMP may provide:

- Executive management
- Administration
- Operations support
- Compliance coordination
- Program coordination
- Contractor coordination
- Vendor management
- Bookkeeping coordination
- Marketing coordination
- Strategic planning
- Technology support
- Event support
- Grant and funding support
- Client reporting

All services must be governed by written agreements, reasonable pricing, clear scopes of work, and appropriate documentation.

## Role of Service Providers

Service providers contribute specialized labor, skill, knowledge, or capacity to the network.

They may be:

- Employees
- Contractors
- Vendors
- Consultants
- Family-owned businesses
- Independent small businesses
- Professional firms

Service providers are expected to maintain appropriate records, deliver actual value, and avoid claiming independence when they are functioning as employees under labor or tax rules.

## Nonprofit / For-Profit Separation

The network may include both nonprofit and for-profit participants. This is acceptable only if their purposes, funds, records, and decision-making remain clear.

Nonprofit funds must serve the nonprofit mission.

For-profit funds may support business operations, contractor payments, wages, reinvestment, and lawful profit.

No nonprofit organization may be used for private inurement, improper private benefit, hidden compensation, or unrelated personal spending.

## Prosperity Ladder

The network should create a ladder of opportunity:

1. Volunteer
2. Part-time worker
3. Employee
4. Independent contractor
5. Small service provider
6. Preferred provider
7. Strategic partner
8. Potential equity participant where legally appropriate
9. Mentor for future providers

Not everyone will move through every level. The point is to create pathways, not promises.

## Long-Term Direction

As the network matures, Verdant may develop:

- A formal provider network
- Preferred vendor standards
- Mentorship systems
- Shared administrative tools
- Business credit pathways
- Training systems
- Strategic partnership agreements
- Future vesting or equity participation models
- Verdant Holdings LLC for asset, acquisition, IP, or exit planning

These future structures should only be created when there is a real legal, financial, and operational reason.

## Foundational Rule

Do not hide money. Structure it correctly.

## Second Rule

VMP succeeds by helping its clients and providers become stronger, more independent, and more financially credible.
