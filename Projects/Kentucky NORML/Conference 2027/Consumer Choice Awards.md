# Consumer Choice Awards — Implementation Plan

**Program Owner:** Kentucky NORML  
**First Year:** 2027  
**Platform:** WordPress-based directory with custom voting module  
**Budget:** $2,000-$4,000 (year 1 build + hosting)

---

## Executive Summary

The Consumer Choice Awards program positions Kentucky NORML as the voice of cannabis consumers while driving traffic to the existing directory. Awards are nominated and voted on by Kentucky consumers, creating an annual cycle of engagement that deepens over time.

**Key Success Metrics (Year 1):**
- 100+ nominations across all categories
- 500+ votes cast
- 5+ sponsorship deals
- 80%+ business/awards recipient satisfaction
- 200+ new directory directory users

---

## Program Structure

### Voting Timeline (Annual Cycle)

**November 1 - December 15:** Nominations
- Open nomination period
- Consumers nominate favorite businesses
- Kentucky NORML verifies nominees

**January 1 - January 31:** Voting
- Public voting opens
- Directory-driven voting (find businesses, read reviews, vote)
- Social media campaign + email reminders
- Voting sponsor messaging

**February 1 - February 28:** Results & Promotion
- Voting closes
- Results announced privately to winners
- Press releases prepared
- Winner interviews/testimonials collected

**March 15-17:** Awards Ceremony
- Presented during Kentucky Cannabis Conference
- Hosted awards reception
- Media coverage
- Social media celebration

14. Best Cultivator
15. Best Processor
16. Best Cannabis Brand
17. Best Packaging

**Note:** Product and industry categories can expand as market matures. Start with consumer-focused categories in Year 1.

### Nomination Process

**Eligible Nominees:**
- Licensed businesses in Kentucky
- Community advocates (individual nominees)
- Educators/organizations

**Ineligible:**
- Closed/unlicensed businesses
- Businesses with regulatory violations in past 12 months
- Self-nominations in consumer categories

**Verification Process:**
1. Consumer submits nomination
2. Kentucky NORML verifies business is licensed (via Department of Health database)
3. Confirms no current regulatory violations
4. Marks as "verified" in voting system
5. Notifies nominated business (optional, to build awareness)

## Risk Management

### Risk: Low Voting Participation

**Mitigation:**
- Build voting into conference registration flow
- Aggressive email and social promotion
- Gamification (leaderboards, challenges)
- Contingency: Extend voting period by 1 week

### Risk: Controversial Voting Outcomes

**Mitigation:**
- Clear nomination criteria
- Verification process prevents obvious manipulation
- Community categories (Advocate of the Year) less controversial
- Stance: "Consumer choice is always valid"

### Risk: Nominated Business Disputes Win Criteria

**Mitigation:**
- Clear program rules published upfront
- Transparent voting tallies provided to winners
- No judging or appeals process (voting is final)
- Communication emphasizes consumer voice, not expert judges

### Risk: Voting System Technical Issues

**Mitigation:**
- Full testing in November-December
- Backup manual voting system (Google Form + spreadsheet)
- Daily backups and monitoring
- 24-hour support person designated

### Risk: Sponsor Expectation Misalignment

**Mitigation:**
- Clear expectations in sponsorship prospectus
- Regular sponsor check-ins
- Performance metrics provided post-event
- Year 2 improvements based on feedback
