# When You Don't Know What to Do: Daily Agenda Guide

**Don't know where to start your day?** Use this flowchart to figure out what matters *right now*.

---

## The 5-Minute Morning Decision Flow

### Step 1: Do the Physical Stuff First (2 min)
```
Morning Routine Minimum:
☐ Hydrate (water)
☐ Eat something
☐ Move your body (stretch/walk)
☐ Check your phone for overnight emergencies
```

*Why: Your brain works better when you're not hungry, dehydrated, or stiff.*

---

### Step 2: Check the Three Systems (2 min)

**Family Status** → Open [[Family Hub]]
- Did anything happen overnight with Matthew, kids, or household?
- Is there a time-sensitive need? (appointment, illness, need)

**Health Alert** → Open [[Lauren Health Log]]
- Any health issue that changes today's priorities?
- Medication/wellness on track?

**Work/Business Status** → Check [[priorities]]
- Any pressing deadline or urgent request?

---

### Step 3: Pick Today's Focus (1 min)

Go to [[Today's Agenda]] and fill in:

```
Priority 1: [The ONE thing that matters most today]
Priority 2: [What should happen if you have time/energy]
Priority 3: [Nice-to-have if the day is generous]
```

**Decision rule:** 
- If there's a family/health emergency → that's Priority 1
- If there's a project deadline → that's Priority 1
- If there's a relationship that needs tending (Matthew, kids, key person) → that's Priority 1
- Otherwise → pick from your current [[priorities]] list

---

## Stuck? Use This Decision Tree

### "There's an emergency"
→ Stop. Handle it. Everything else pauses. Your family comes first.

### "I have a project deadline"
→ That's your Priority 1. Block time for it.

### "Nothing feels urgent"
→ Pick **one** from these areas (in order of importance today):
1. Family/kids/Matthew support needed?
2. Health or wellness issue?
3. Business operations (see [[Office]])
4. System maintenance (like this vault)

### "I don't know which project matters"
→ Open [[priorities]] and [[Office/Revenue Engines/! Revenue Engine List.md]]
- Which revenue stream needs attention?
- Which project has the next milestone?
- What was you going to do last week?

### "I'm overwhelmed"
→ **Pick just ONE task.** Not three. Not "reorganize." ONE concrete thing.
Examples:
- "Make one call to [person]"
- "Work on [project] for 30 minutes"
- "Fix [one specific thing]"
- "Have a check-in with Matthew"

## The Daily Rhythm

### Morning (8-10am)
- Morning routine
- Scan the three systems
- Pick today's priorities
- Start Priority 1

### Mid-Day (12-2pm)
- Check in with family/Matthew
- Adjust if needed
- Energy check (getting tired? hungry? need a break?)

### Afternoon (2-5pm)
- Complete Priority 1 or 2
- Triage any new requests
- Plan what's for dinner / household

### Evening (5-7pm)
- Wind down from work
- Family time
- Evening routine
- Quick tomorrow prep

---

## Weekly Anchor Points

### Every Sunday or Monday
- Review [[priorities]]
- Check upcoming calendar (Matthew, kids activities)
- Note revenue/project milestones for the week
- Plan your weekly focus areas

### Every Friday Evening
- [[Weekly Reset]] — what worked? what didn't?
- Prep for next week
- Clear desk/inbox

## The Shortcut: When You're Really Stuck

If you have **less than 5 minutes** and your brain won't cooperate, pick from this list in order:

1. **Check Family Hub** — is there a need?
2. **Check your top 3 projects** — which one is due soon?
3. **Check your health** — is something off?
4. **Default:** Spend 30 minutes on whichever of your revenue engines hasn't gotten attention this week

## Remember

- **You can't do everything today.** Pick 1-3 things.
- **Your family comes first.** Always.
- **If you're stuck, you're usually tired, hungry, or unclear.** Eat, hydrate, ask for help.
- **Tomorrow is another day.** Whatever you don't finish today will still be there.
